You know your accounting firm needs automation. The question is whether to set it up yourself or have someone do it for you.

Both approaches work. Neither is universally better. The right choice depends on your firm's situation, and being honest about it will save you time and money.

The DIY Approach

When DIY Makes Sense

  • You or someone on your team genuinely enjoys technology and has time to learn new platforms
  • You are implementing one simple tool, not a connected stack
  • You have a slow period coming up where setup will not compete with client work
  • Your budget is tight and you have more time than money right now

The Real Cost of DIY

The subscription cost of most automation tools is not the expensive part. The expensive part is your time.

A typical CRM and marketing automation setup takes 40 to 80 hours to do properly. That includes:

  • Learning the platform (10 to 20 hours)
  • Planning your workflows and sequences (5 to 10 hours)
  • Building and testing everything (15 to 30 hours)
  • Fixing what breaks after launch (10 to 20 hours)

If your effective hourly rate is 150 dollars or more, that is 6,000 to 12,000 dollars in opportunity cost. And that assumes you finish, which based on what we see, about half of DIY implementations stall before they are complete.

Common DIY Pitfalls

  • The busy season pause: You start setup in October, busy season hits, and you do not touch it again until June. By then the platform has changed and you are starting over.
  • The 30 percent problem: You get the basics working but never build the advanced automations that deliver the real value. You are paying full price for a tool you barely use.
  • The integration gap: Each tool works on its own but they are not connected. Data does not flow between them, so you are still doing manual work to bridge the gaps.
  • Template dependence: You use the default templates instead of customizing for your practice. The results are generic because the setup is generic.

The Done For You Approach

When Done For You Makes Sense

  • Your team's time is better spent on billable client work
  • You need multiple tools working together as a system
  • You have tried DIY before and it did not get finished
  • You want results on a predictable timeline
  • You do not want to become an expert in marketing or automation software

What Done For You Actually Means

A good done for you service handles:

  • Strategy: Figuring out which tools and workflows will actually move the needle for your specific practice
  • Implementation: All technical setup, configuration, integration, and testing
  • Customization: Building workflows around how your firm actually operates, not generic templates
  • Training: Showing your team what they need to know without overwhelming them
  • Ongoing management: Monitoring, optimizing, and maintaining systems so they keep working

What to Watch Out For

Not all done for you services are the same. Red flags to look for:

  • Long term contracts before you have seen results
  • No accounting industry experience (general marketing agencies often miss the nuances)
  • Vague deliverables or metrics
  • Cookie cutter solutions that do not account for your specific practice

A Simple Decision Framework

Ask yourself these three questions:

1. Do I have someone who will actually finish the setup?

Be honest. Not someone who wants to, someone who will. If you have started and stalled on automation projects before, that is your answer.

2. Is my time worth more than the cost of implementation help?

If your billable rate times the hours you would spend on setup exceeds the cost of done for you implementation, the math speaks for itself.

3. Do I need one tool or a connected system?

Setting up one tool yourself is manageable. Building an integrated system where your CRM talks to your email platform talks to your scheduling tool talks to your client portal? That is where DIY typically falls apart.
