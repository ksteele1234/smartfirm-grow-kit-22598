## The Execution Gap

The difference between strategy and results is execution. Many accounting firms have received excellent advice about what they should be doing for marketing and operations. The problem is rarely the advice. It is the execution.

**Firm owners do not have time to implement.** You run a practice. You serve clients. You manage staff. You handle compliance. Adding "build a marketing automation system" or "configure a CRM" to that list means it either does not happen or gets done poorly between other priorities. Done-for-you means the implementation happens without taking your time.

**Coaching assumes you will do the work.** Coaching programs provide knowledge, frameworks, and accountability. That works well if the bottleneck is knowledge. For most firm owners, the bottleneck is time and technical skill, not knowledge. You understand that you need marketing automation. You need someone to build it, not explain it again.

**Templates require customization.** A template for an email nurture sequence is a starting point, not a finished product. It needs to be customized for your firm, loaded into your platform, tested, and launched. Each template represents 2 to 4 hours of work that rarely happens. Done-for-you means the templates are customized, loaded, tested, and running.

**DIY marketing tools require ongoing management.** Even if the initial setup gets done, marketing automation requires ongoing attention: monitoring performance, adjusting sequences, updating content, troubleshooting integrations. DIY means you manage this indefinitely. Done-for-you means someone else handles the ongoing management.
