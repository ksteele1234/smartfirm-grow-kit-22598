## What Canopy Handles

Canopy has carved out a niche in the accounting practice management space, particularly for firms with tax resolution work. Recognizing its strengths helps clarify what it leaves unaddressed.

**Tax resolution workflow management.** Canopy's tax resolution features help firms manage the complex, multi-step process of resolving tax issues with the IRS and state agencies. Transcript analysis, notice tracking, and resolution workflow management are specific capabilities that set Canopy apart from more general practice management tools.

**Document management and client portal.** Canopy provides a client portal for document exchange and a document management system for organizing client files. Tax firms that need secure document collection and storage find these features useful for managing the volume of documents that tax work generates.

**Practice management fundamentals.** Task management, time tracking, billing, and client records provide the operational foundation that any practice management tool needs to offer. Canopy handles the basics of running a tax practice.

**Client communication within the portal.** The client portal enables secure messaging and document sharing, keeping sensitive tax information out of email and providing a centralized communication channel for active client work.

## Where Tax Firms Hit the Growth Ceiling

Practice management tools optimize the work you do. They do not create demand for your services or bring new clients to your firm.

**No client acquisition system.** Canopy does not capture website leads, run email marketing campaigns, manage social media, or automate the follow-up sequences that convert inquiries into consultations. The entire front end of the client relationship happens outside the platform.

**No seasonal marketing automation.** Tax firms live and die by their ability to fill their capacity before and during tax season. Pre-season marketing, early-bird campaigns, deadline reminder outreach, and extension-season acquisition all require marketing automation that Canopy does not provide.

**No prospect nurture pipeline.** Someone visits your website, fills out a contact form, and then what? Without automated follow-up, that prospect waits for a manual response. If you are busy with existing clients, that response comes late, or not at all. Canopy has no mechanism for managing the prospect journey before someone becomes an active client.

**No reputation or referral management.** Collecting Google reviews, encouraging client referrals, and managing your online presence are growth activities that require their own automation. Tax firms that depend on word of mouth still benefit from systematizing how referrals are requested and how the firm's online reputation is maintained.

**No retention automation between tax seasons.** The period between April and the following January is when client relationships are most at risk. Clients who do not hear from their tax preparer for nine months are more likely to shop around when the next season approaches. Canopy does not automate the off-season communication that maintains these relationships.
