## The ROI Visibility Problem

Most accounting firms cannot answer a simple question: for every dollar you spend on marketing, how many dollars come back in new client revenue? The inability to answer this question is not a failure of the firm. It is a failure of the marketing systems.

**Website-only providers do not track beyond the website.** A website provider can tell you how many people visited your site. They might tell you which pages were viewed and how long visitors stayed. But they typically cannot tell you which visitors became leads, which leads became clients, and how much revenue those clients represent. The tracking stops at the website rather than following the prospect through to revenue.

**Marketing activity is reported, not marketing results.** Common marketing reports show activity: emails sent, social posts published, ads run. Activity metrics feel productive but they do not answer the question that matters: did these activities produce revenue?

**Without ROI data, you cannot optimize spending.** If you run three marketing channels and do not know which one produces the most revenue per dollar spent, you are allocating budget based on intuition rather than data.

**Many firms overspend on low-return channels.** Without transparent ROI, firms often continue spending on channels that feel productive but do not actually convert leads into clients.

## How SmartFirm Tracks ROI

**Lead source attribution.** Every lead that enters your system is tagged with its source: website form, Google search, social media, referral, email campaign, or paid advertising. The source stays with the lead through the entire lifecycle so that when they become a client, the originating source is connected to the revenue they generate.

**Conversion tracking through the funnel.** Leads are tracked through each stage: inquiry received, follow-up completed, consultation scheduled, proposal sent, engagement signed. You see where in the funnel leads convert and where they drop off.

**Revenue attribution.** When a lead becomes a client, the revenue from that engagement is attributed to the original marketing source. You see not just how many leads each channel produces, but how much revenue those leads generate over time.

**Cost-per-acquisition by channel.** Your marketing spend is divided by the number of clients acquired from each channel, showing the actual cost to acquire a client through each source.

**Dashboard with real-time metrics.** A marketing dashboard shows the current state of lead generation, conversion, and ROI. Metrics update as leads progress through the funnel. You do not wait for a monthly report to understand performance.

**Periodic reporting with insights.** Beyond the dashboard, periodic reports analyze trends, compare periods, and identify opportunities for improvement.

## Your Questions Answered

**How accurate is the ROI tracking?**
The tracking is based on actual lead data and actual revenue data from your systems. Attribution is as accurate as the data allows: leads that come through tracked channels (web forms, ad clicks, email links) are attributed precisely. Leads that come through untracked channels (phone calls without tracking numbers, walk-in referrals) can be tagged manually.

**What if we do not have a large marketing budget?**
ROI tracking is especially valuable for firms with limited budgets because every dollar matters more. Knowing which channels produce the best return allows you to concentrate a small budget where it is most effective.

**Can you track ROI for marketing we were already doing before SmartFirm?**
Going forward, yes. The tracking applies to all marketing activity from the point of implementation. Most firms find that forward-looking tracking provides enough data to make informed decisions within the first few months.

**Does the tracking work with referral-based growth?**
Yes. Referral programs can be tracked by tagging referred leads with the referring source. You see how many referrals each client or referral partner generates and how those referrals convert.
