## Why Kartra Does Not Fit Accounting

Kartra is a well-built platform for its intended audience. Accounting firms are not that audience.

**Kartra's model is digital product sales.** Kartra is designed around a funnel: attract attention, capture an email, nurture with a sequence, sell a product, deliver digitally, upsell to the next product. Accounting firms do not sell this way. Accounting clients research, evaluate trust signals, have a conversation, decide based on relationship fit, and engage for ongoing services. The funnel model does not match the accounting client journey.

**The features you need are missing.** Kartra does not include practice management integration, client onboarding workflows for professional services, engagement tracking, document collection, or accounting-specific communication templates. These capabilities are essential for an accounting firm and would need to be built entirely outside of Kartra.

**The features it offers are irrelevant.** Membership sites, course hosting, video hosting, checkout pages for digital products, and affiliate management are core Kartra features that an accounting firm will never use. Paying for and navigating around irrelevant features adds cost and complexity without value.

**Accounting clients expect a different experience.** A coaching client expects a funnel, a sales page, and a checkout button. An accounting client expects a professional website, a consultative conversation, and an engagement letter. Using tools designed for the first experience to create the second results in a client journey that feels wrong, even if the technical pieces work.
