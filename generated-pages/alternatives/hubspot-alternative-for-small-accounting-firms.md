## Why HubSpot Is Not Right for Most Small Accounting Firms

HubSpot is an excellent platform for businesses it was designed for. Small accounting firms are not that audience.

**The pricing scales beyond what small firms can justify.** HubSpot's free tier is limited. The features that accounting firms need (automation workflows, sequences, custom reporting) require paid tiers that escalate quickly. For a firm with $500K to $1.5M in revenue, HubSpot's monthly cost can be difficult to justify, especially when much of what you pay for is unused.

**The feature set is overwhelming.** HubSpot offers hundreds of features across marketing, sales, service, and CMS hubs. A small accounting firm needs 10 to 15 of those features and will never touch the rest. Navigating the platform to find and use the relevant features requires learning a system that is far larger than what you need.

**It requires someone to manage it.** HubSpot is designed to be managed by a marketing team or a dedicated HubSpot administrator. Small accounting firms do not have either. Without ongoing management, HubSpot setups degrade: contacts go uncleaned, sequences go unupdated, and the CRM becomes a data repository that no one uses effectively.

**It is not designed for accounting.** HubSpot serves all industries. It does not come with accounting-specific templates, workflows, or configurations. Everything must be custom-built for the accounting context: seasonal campaigns, tax-related communication, compliance-aware messaging, and accounting-specific client journeys. Building these from scratch requires time and accounting industry knowledge that the platform does not provide.
