## What Karbon Does Well

Karbon is purpose-built for accounting practice management, and it shows. Understanding what Karbon handles well clarifies where the gap exists.

**Workflow management for recurring work.** Karbon excels at managing the repeatable engagements that define accounting: tax returns, monthly bookkeeping, annual audits. Templates, task assignments, and due dates keep recurring work on track across the team.

**Email triage and client communication tracking.** Karbon's email integration captures client communication and associates it with work items. For firms drowning in email, this organization is valuable. Staff can see the full communication history for any engagement without searching through individual inboxes.

**Team collaboration and task management.** Task assignment, status tracking, and workload visibility help managers distribute work and monitor progress. For multi-person firms, this coordination is essential during busy periods.

**Accounting-specific design.** Unlike generic project management tools, Karbon understands accounting terminology, engagement types, and seasonal patterns. The platform speaks the language of accounting practices.

---

## The Gap Karbon Does Not Fill

Practice management assumes you have clients to manage. It does not address how you get those clients or how you systematically grow the practice.

**No marketing automation.** Karbon does not send marketing emails, manage lead nurture sequences, run social media, capture website leads, or automate referral programs. The entire client acquisition process happens outside the platform.

**No lead management.** When a prospect contacts your firm, Karbon has no mechanism to track that prospect through the evaluation and conversion process. Prospects are not clients yet, and Karbon is designed for client work, not prospect management.

**No online presence management.** Review collection, Google Business Profile optimization, social media scheduling, and reputation management are not part of the Karbon platform. These growth activities require separate tools and separate management.

**No client lifecycle automation beyond active work.** The period between when a client's annual engagement ends and when the next one begins is critical for retention. Karbon does not automate the check-in emails, satisfaction surveys, referral requests, or seasonal updates that keep clients engaged between active work periods.

**No new client onboarding automation.** When a prospect becomes a client, the transition from prospect to active engagement involves intake forms, engagement letters, document collection, and welcome communication. This onboarding sequence is different from the recurring workflow templates that Karbon manages.
