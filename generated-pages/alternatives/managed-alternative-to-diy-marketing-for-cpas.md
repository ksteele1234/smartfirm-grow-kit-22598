## The DIY Marketing Trap

DIY marketing is appealing in theory: lower cost, full control, learn a valuable skill. In practice, it fails for most CPAs for predictable reasons.

**Your primary skill is accounting, not marketing.** You spent years developing expertise in tax, audit, or advisory. Marketing requires a different skill set: copywriting, design, analytics, platform management, and campaign strategy. Learning marketing well enough to be effective takes significant time, and the learning never truly stops because platforms and best practices evolve constantly.

**Your time is worth more doing accounting.** Every hour you spend on marketing is an hour not spent on billable work or practice management. If your billable rate is $200 to $400 per hour, the opportunity cost of DIY marketing is substantial. A managed service costs a fraction of what your time is worth.

**Consistency is the challenge, not capability.** You might be perfectly capable of writing a newsletter, posting on social media, or configuring an email sequence. The challenge is doing these things consistently, week after week, month after month, during busy season and slow season. DIY marketing typically starts strong and fades as client work takes priority.

**DIY creates a patchwork of tools.** Without a unified strategy, DIY marketing accumulates separate tools: an email platform, a social media scheduler, a contact list in a spreadsheet, a website without lead capture, and reviews managed through individual responses. Each tool works in isolation. None of them connect. Managed marketing unifies these into a system.

**Failed DIY attempts have a cost.** Abandoned platform subscriptions, wasted setup time, and the months of missed marketing opportunities all have a real cost. Firms that have been through two or three DIY cycles have often spent more in time and abandoned tools than a managed service would have cost.
