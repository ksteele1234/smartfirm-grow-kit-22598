## Coaching Versus Implementation

Coaching and implementation serve different needs, and understanding the difference helps you choose the right approach for your firm.

**Coaching builds knowledge. Implementation builds systems.** Coaching teaches you what to do, why to do it, and how to approach it. Implementation actually does it. Both are valuable, but they solve different problems. If you already understand what your firm needs and the bottleneck is getting it done, implementation is the more direct path to results.

**Coaching requires your time for execution. Implementation does not.** After a coaching session, you return to your practice with a list of action items. Those items compete with client work, compliance deadlines, and staff management for your attention. Implementation happens in parallel with your practice operations because someone else is doing the work.

**Coaching programs often stop before the technical work.** A coaching program might help you design your ideal client onboarding process. But configuring the forms, setting up the e-signature routing, building the email sequence, and integrating with your practice management system requires technical implementation work that is outside the coaching scope. SmartFirm handles the full technical build.

**Results from coaching depend on follow-through. Results from implementation depend on the implementation team.** If you have excellent follow-through, coaching may be sufficient. If your firm consistently plans well but executes slowly, done-for-you implementation removes the dependency on your personal bandwidth.
