## The Scheduling Problem at CPA Firms

CPA firms schedule meetings constantly. New client consultations, annual tax planning sessions, quarterly reviews, return pickup appointments, and ad hoc discussions about client questions. Each of these meetings currently involves some version of the same frustrating process:

**The email back-and-forth.** "Are you available Tuesday at 2?" "No, how about Wednesday?" "Wednesday does not work. Thursday?" This exchange takes three to five emails on average and might span several days. Multiply this by the number of meetings each partner and staff member schedules per week, and the administrative time is substantial.

**The calendar juggling.** Partners often have multiple calendars (personal, firm, client-facing) that are not synchronized. Double-booking happens. Meetings get scheduled during blocked preparation time. Administrative staff spend time cross-referencing calendars to find open slots.

**The no-show problem.** A client books a meeting (or your team schedules one), and the client does not show up. The partner cleared time, prepared materials, and was ready. That time is now lost. In many firms, no-shows happen frequently enough to be a recurring source of frustration.

**Seasonal scheduling complexity.** During busy season, partner availability is extremely limited. Scheduling becomes harder, appointments get pushed further out, and clients feel deprioritized. During slower months, the opposite can happen: the schedule is open but clients do not know it because booking still requires calling the office or sending an email.

## How Automated Scheduling Works

An automated scheduling system addresses each of these problems:

**Self-service booking.** Clients access your scheduling page (linked from your website, email signature, or client portal) and choose from your available time slots. No emails. No phone calls. No back-and-forth. They see what is available and book directly.

**Calendar integration.** Your scheduling system connects to your calendar so it only shows truly available time. When you block time for preparation, those slots are not offered. When a meeting is booked elsewhere, that time is automatically removed from availability. No double-booking.

**Appointment type configuration.** Different meetings have different requirements. A new client consultation might need 60 minutes and only be available during certain hours. A return review might need 30 minutes. A tax planning session might need 90 minutes and only be available with specific partners. The scheduling system offers the right options based on the appointment type.

**Pre-meeting information.** When a client books, the system can request information needed for the meeting: the purpose of the appointment, relevant documents to bring, specific questions they want to discuss. This turns the booking action into a preparation step that makes the meeting itself more productive.

**Automated reminders.** After booking, the client receives confirmation immediately. Then automated reminders go out at intervals you define: one week before, one day before, one hour before. Each reminder includes the meeting details, any preparation instructions, and an easy way to reschedule if needed.

## The Reminder Sequence That Reduces No-Shows

Automated reminders are not just a single "reminder: you have a meeting tomorrow" message. An effective reminder sequence for CPA appointments includes several touchpoints:

**Booking confirmation.** Immediately after the client books, they receive a confirmation with the date, time, location (or video link), and a summary of what the meeting will cover. This confirmation also includes any pre-meeting actions the client should take.

**Preparation reminder.** A few days before the meeting, a reminder prompts the client to prepare: gather documents, review their financials, write down questions. For tax appointments, this might include a checklist of items to bring. Clients who prepare make better use of the meeting time.

**Day-before reminder.** A reminder the day before confirms the appointment and provides logistics: office address, parking instructions, or the video conference link. This is when most clients who need to reschedule will do so, giving you time to fill the slot.

**Day-of reminder.** A brief reminder on the morning of the appointment (or an hour before) serves as the final confirmation. This catches clients who may have forgotten despite earlier reminders.

**Post-meeting follow up.** After the appointment, an automated message thanks the client, summarizes any action items discussed, and provides links to relevant resources or next steps. This extends the value of the meeting beyond the conversation itself.

## Your Questions Answered

**Will clients actually use online scheduling, or will they still call the office?**
Most clients prefer online scheduling once they try it, especially younger business owners who are accustomed to booking everything online. For clients who prefer calling, your office can still book appointments directly. The system supports both approaches. Over time, as more clients use self-service booking, the administrative burden decreases.

**Can we control exactly when clients can book, or will they fill our entire calendar?**
You have full control over availability. You set which hours are bookable, how far in advance clients can schedule, buffer time between appointments, and daily or weekly booking limits. The system shows clients only the time slots you have made available, protecting your preparation time and personal boundaries.

**How do automated reminders work with clients who prefer phone calls over email?**
Reminder sequences can use multiple channels: email, text messages, or both. Many clients respond better to text reminders because they are harder to overlook than emails. The specific channels are configured based on your clients' preferences and the information they provide when booking.

**What happens when a client needs to reschedule?**
Every reminder includes a reschedule link that lets the client choose a new time without calling your office. The system automatically updates your calendar, notifies relevant team members, and adjusts the reminder sequence for the new appointment. Rescheduling is as easy as booking.
