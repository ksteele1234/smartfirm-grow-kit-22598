## The Onboarding Problem at Accounting Firms

Client onboarding at most accounting firms follows a familiar pattern: someone remembers some of the steps, improvises the rest, and every new client gets a slightly different experience depending on who handled their setup and how busy the firm was that week.

This creates several problems:

**Inconsistent first impressions.** The first few weeks of a client relationship set the tone for everything that follows. When one client receives a polished welcome package within hours and another waits three days for a disorganized email asking for documents, the firm's reputation varies by accident.

**Document collection delays.** New clients need to provide significant documentation: prior year returns, financial statements, entity documents, bank access, software credentials. When this collection is manual, items get forgotten, requests go to the wrong person, and the engagement cannot begin until someone chases down the missing pieces.

**Team coordination gaps.** Onboarding involves multiple people: the partner who sold the engagement, the manager who will oversee it, the staff who will do the work, and potentially an admin who handles billing and access. Without a structured workflow, tasks fall between people and things get missed.

**Wasted professional time.** Partners and managers spend time on administrative onboarding tasks (sending welcome emails, following up on documents, setting up billing) that could be handled by a system. This time is not billable and does not require their expertise.

## What Automated Onboarding Looks Like

An automated onboarding system handles the administrative steps of bringing on a new client, triggering each step based on the previous one completing:

**Immediate welcome.** When a new client is entered into the system, they receive a professional welcome communication that introduces the team, explains next steps, and sets expectations for the onboarding timeline.

**Engagement letter delivery.** The system generates and sends the engagement letter based on the services agreed upon. E-signature collection is handled automatically with reminders for unsigned documents.

**Document request sequence.** A structured document request goes to the client with a clear list of what is needed, why it is needed, and how to submit it. Automated follow ups ensure nothing falls through the cracks without your team manually tracking who has submitted what.

**Internal task assignment.** As client-facing steps complete, internal tasks are triggered: CRM record setup, billing configuration, software access provisioning, team assignment notification. Each person involved knows what they need to do and when.

**Progress tracking.** The system tracks where each new client is in the onboarding process. If a client stalls at the document collection stage, the system sends reminders and alerts your team if intervention is needed.

**Kickoff scheduling.** Once the administrative steps are complete, the system prompts the client to schedule their kickoff meeting, ensuring the engagement begins with all paperwork handled and the team prepared.
