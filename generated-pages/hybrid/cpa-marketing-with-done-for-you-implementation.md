## The Implementation Gap

There is a pattern that repeats across CPA firms of all sizes. It usually looks something like this:

The firm decides they need to invest in marketing. They research options. They attend a webinar. They sign up for a tool or platform. Maybe it is a CRM. Maybe it is an email marketing platform. Maybe it is a full marketing automation suite.

Then nothing happens.

The tool requires configuration. Someone needs to set up email templates, build landing pages, create automation workflows, connect integrations, import contacts, and test everything. That someone is supposed to be a partner or office manager who already has a full workload.

The tool sits in the "we will get to it" pile. A few months later, the firm is still paying for software nobody uses. They cancel, conclude that "marketing does not work for us," and go back to relying entirely on referrals.

The problem was never the tool. It was the implementation.

## Why CPAs Specifically Need Done For You

CPAs face a particular challenge with marketing implementation that other professionals do not. Several factors make DIY marketing especially impractical for accounting firms:

**Billable hour economics.** A partner's time has a direct dollar value. Every hour spent configuring marketing software is an hour not spent on billable client work. The opportunity cost makes DIY implementation expensive even before considering whether the partner is good at marketing technology.

**Seasonal workload compression.** CPA firms have defined busy periods where capacity drops to zero for non-client work. A DIY marketing project that starts in November is abandoned by January. The system sits half-built until June, by which point the context is lost and the project starts over.

**Technical skill mismatch.** CPAs are skilled at accounting, tax, and advisory work. Marketing technology requires a different skill set: automation logic, email deliverability, conversion optimization, analytics interpretation. These are learnable skills, but learning them is not a good use of a CPA's time.

**Perfectionism and delegation resistance.** Many CPAs struggle to delegate work they do not fully understand. With DIY marketing, this means they want to understand every setting, review every email, and approve every workflow before it launches. This is reasonable quality control. But it also means nothing ever launches because there is always one more thing to review.

Done for you implementation addresses all of these by removing the firm's team from the technical work entirely. You set the direction. We handle everything else.

## Your Questions Answered

**How is this different from hiring a marketing agency?**
Most marketing agencies design strategy and create campaigns, but expect the client to manage the technology. They deliver a plan or creative assets, and you are responsible for implementation. Done for you means we handle both the strategy and the technical execution. We build the systems, configure the tools, create the content, and manage everything on an ongoing basis.

**What if we already have marketing tools we are paying for but not using?**
This is one of the most common situations we encounter. During discovery, we assess what you already have and determine what is usable, what needs reconfiguration, and what should be replaced. In many cases, the tools you already own can be made effective with proper setup and management.

**How much involvement does our team need during implementation?**
Your involvement is primarily in the discovery and direction-setting phase. We need to understand your firm, your clients, and your preferences. After that, the technical work is ours. We will check in with you at key milestones and before anything launches, but the day to day implementation does not require your team's time.

**What happens if we want to bring marketing in house eventually?**
The systems we build are yours. If at some point you hire a marketing coordinator or decide to manage things internally, we can transition the management and provide documentation and training. Done for you does not mean locked in.
