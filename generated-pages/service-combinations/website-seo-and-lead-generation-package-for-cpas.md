## The Three-Legged Stool

Online client acquisition for CPA firms depends on three components that each need the others to function:

**The website: your conversion platform.** A CPA firm's website needs to do more than list services and display partner photos. It needs to communicate value, address prospect concerns, present clear calls to action, and make it easy to take the next step. A website that informs but does not convert is a brochure, not a business development tool.

**SEO: your traffic engine.** Search engine optimization makes your website visible when business owners search for CPA services in your market. Without SEO, your website depends entirely on direct navigation (people who already know your firm) and referral traffic. With SEO, your website attracts a continuous stream of business owners who are actively looking for what you offer.

**Lead generation: your conversion mechanism.** Lead generation turns website visitors into contacts your firm can follow up with. This includes the forms, scheduling tools, content offers, and follow up sequences that capture prospect information and nurture it into a consultation. Without lead generation, even a well-designed, well-ranked website is just attracting visitors who leave without taking action.

When one leg is weak or missing, the stool does not stand. The package addresses all three together.

## What Each Component Contributes

**Website design and optimization:**
- Professional design that reflects your firm's positioning and expertise
- Service pages structured to address the specific concerns of your target clients
- Clear calls to action on every page that guide visitors toward engagement
- Mobile optimization because a significant portion of prospect research happens on phones
- Fast load times and technical performance that support both user experience and search rankings
- Content structure that serves both human visitors and search engines

**Search engine optimization:**
- Keyword research targeting the search terms your ideal clients use ("CPA for real estate investors," "small business tax planning [your city]," etc.)
- On-page optimization so each page targets specific search intent
- Local SEO for geographic visibility in your market
- Content strategy that builds topical authority in your service areas
- Ongoing optimization based on ranking performance and search trends

**Lead generation:**
- Strategic lead capture forms on high-value pages
- Downloadable resources (guides, checklists, planning tools) that provide value in exchange for contact information
- Consultation scheduling integrated directly into the website
- Automated follow up sequences for different lead types (download leads, inquiry leads, scheduling leads)
- Lead qualification so your team's time is focused on the most promising prospects
- CRM integration so captured leads are tracked and managed

## How the Three Components Reinforce Each Other

When all three are built as one package, they create reinforcing dynamics:

**SEO drives relevant traffic to conversion-ready pages.** Your website is not just optimized for search rankings. It is optimized for the specific searches that indicate buying intent. Visitors who arrive from these searches are already looking for CPA services, which means they are more likely to engage with your lead generation elements.

**Website content serves both SEO and lead generation.** A blog post about year-end tax strategies ranks in search (bringing traffic), provides value to visitors (building trust), and includes a call to action for a related downloadable guide (capturing leads). One piece of content serves all three functions.

**Lead generation data improves SEO strategy.** When you track which search terms produce the most leads (not just the most traffic), you can refine your SEO strategy to focus on the terms that generate business outcomes. This feedback loop makes the SEO investment more efficient over time.

**The website converts traffic from every source.** While SEO provides organic search traffic, your website also receives traffic from referrals, social media, directory listings, and direct navigation. The lead generation system converts visitors regardless of how they arrived, maximizing the return on every traffic source.

**Do we need a new website, or can we optimize our existing one?**
It depends on your current website's foundation. During discovery, we assess your website's technical platform, design quality, content structure, and conversion elements. Some websites can be significantly improved through optimization and strategic additions. Others benefit from a redesign. We recommend the approach that delivers the best results for your situation.

**How long does it take for SEO to produce results?**
SEO is a longer-term investment that builds over months. Lead generation and website optimization can produce results more quickly, especially if your website already receives traffic that is not converting. The package is designed so that lead generation captures value immediately while SEO builds the foundation for increasing traffic over time.

**What search terms should a CPA firm target?**
The specific terms depend on your services, target clients, and geographic market. Common targets include service-specific terms ("tax planning for small businesses"), geographic terms ("CPA firm in [your city]"), and audience-specific terms ("accountant for real estate investors"). We conduct keyword research during discovery to identify the terms that match your firm's strengths and target market.

**What if we already have good traffic but are not getting leads?**
That is a strong indicator that the lead generation component is the missing piece. If your website ranks well and receives traffic but visitors leave without taking action, the priority is adding conversion elements (strategic forms, scheduling tools, content offers) and follow up sequences that turn existing traffic into leads.
