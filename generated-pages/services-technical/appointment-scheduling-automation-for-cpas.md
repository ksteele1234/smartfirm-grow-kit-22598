## What Scheduling Automation Includes

A complete scheduling automation system has several interconnected components:

**Self-service booking pages.** Web-based scheduling pages where clients and prospects can see available time slots and book directly. Booking pages can be embedded on your website, linked from emails, or shared as standalone URLs. Each booking page can be configured for different appointment types: initial consultations, tax preparation meetings, quarterly reviews, advisory sessions.

**Availability rules.** Configuration that defines when you are available for meetings: which days, which hours, how far in advance clients can book, minimum time between meetings (buffer time), and maximum meetings per day. Different meeting types can have different availability rules. Tax preparation meetings might be available January through April. Advisory consultations might be available year-round.

**Calendar synchronization.** The scheduling system connects to your existing calendar (Google Calendar, Outlook, or other platforms) and checks availability in real time. If you have a meeting on your calendar, that time is blocked from booking. If you add a personal appointment, scheduling availability updates immediately. Multiple team members can have their own calendars synced.

**Meeting type configuration.** Different appointment types are configured with their own duration, availability, booking page, and communication sequences. An initial prospect consultation (30 minutes, available to anyone, includes a brief intake form) operates differently from a tax preparation meeting (60 minutes, available to existing clients, includes a document preparation reminder).

**Automated confirmations.** When a meeting is booked, the client receives an immediate confirmation with meeting details: date, time, location or video call link, and any preparation instructions. The confirmation is sent automatically, so the client has clear information within seconds of booking.

**Reminder sequences.** Pre-meeting reminders are sent at defined intervals: a week before, a day before, an hour before, or whatever cadence works for your practice. Reminders reduce no-shows and ensure clients arrive prepared. Reminders can include specific preparation instructions relevant to the meeting type.

**Post-meeting follow up.** After the meeting occurs, the system sends follow up communication: a summary of next steps, relevant resources, or a scheduling link for the next meeting. Follow up is triggered by the meeting completion event and tailored to the meeting type.

## Why CPA Firms Need Scheduling Automation

**The email tax.** Every meeting that is scheduled through email requires multiple messages: an offer of times, a response, a confirmation, possibly a reschedule, and a calendar invitation. Across dozens of client meetings per month, the email volume for scheduling alone is significant.

**Seasonal volume spikes.** During tax season, the number of client meetings increases dramatically. The manual scheduling process that works in the off-season becomes a bottleneck when every client needs a meeting within the same few-month window.

**No-shows cost time.** When a client forgets a meeting, the preparer's time is wasted. Automated reminders at appropriate intervals significantly reduce no-shows because the reminder reaches every client consistently, not just the ones someone remembered to remind.

**First impressions matter.** When a prospect books an initial consultation, the booking experience is their first interaction with your firm's operations. A professional, instant, self-service booking process creates a different impression than a multi-day email exchange to find a mutually available time.

## Your Questions Answered

**Does this work with our current calendar system?**
Yes. Scheduling automation integrates with Google Calendar, Outlook, and other major calendar platforms. Your team continues using their existing calendars; the scheduling system reads and writes to them in real time.

**Can different team members have different availability?**
Yes. Each team member can have their own booking page, availability rules, and meeting types. A partner's advisory consultation schedule looks different from a staff accountant's tax preparation meeting schedule. The system accommodates different roles.

**What if a client needs to reschedule?**
The confirmation and reminder emails include a reschedule link. Clients can reschedule on their own through the same self-service system, and the original time slot opens back up for other bookings automatically.

**Can we require information from clients before the meeting?**
Yes. Booking pages can include intake forms that ask clients to provide information or answer questions before confirming the appointment. This ensures you have the context you need before the meeting and helps the client prepare.
