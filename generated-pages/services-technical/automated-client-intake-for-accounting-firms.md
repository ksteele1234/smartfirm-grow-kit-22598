## Why Intake Is the Critical First Step

The intake phase sets the data foundation for the entire client relationship. Information collected during intake populates your CRM, practice management system, tax software, billing platform, and document management system. When intake is incomplete or inaccurate, every downstream system inherits the problems.

**The incomplete data problem.** A new client sends an email with their name, address, and phone number. The admin enters it into the CRM. But the record is missing the entity type, EIN, fiscal year end, prior CPA contact information, and signing authority details. Those fields sit empty until someone notices they need them, which is usually when the engagement work has already begun.

**The formatting inconsistency.** One client writes their business name as "Smith & Associates LLC." Another client's intake form says "Smith and Associates, L.L.C." Without standardized data entry, records have formatting variations that cause problems for reporting, searching, and matching across systems.

**The scattered document problem.** The engagement letter arrives via email. The W-9 is faxed. The prior year returns are uploaded to a file sharing link. The authorization form is mailed. Each document arrives through a different channel, and someone must track what has been received, what is outstanding, and where each document ended up.

**The re-entry cost.** Information collected on paper or in email must be manually re-entered into your practice management system, CRM, and tax software. Re-entry takes time and introduces errors. A mistyped EIN or transposed digits in a Social Security number can cause filing problems that are difficult to trace back to the intake phase.

## What Automated Intake Includes

**Conditional intake forms.** Digital forms that adapt based on the client's answers. An individual client sees fields for Social Security number, filing status, and dependent information. A business entity client sees fields for EIN, entity type, fiscal year end, and officer information. A trust client sees trustee details and beneficiary information. One intake process handles every client type through conditional logic.

**Field validation and formatting.** As data is entered, the system validates it: EIN format, phone number format, email validity, ZIP code verification, and required field completion. Validation prevents common data entry errors at the point of collection rather than discovering them weeks later.

**Document upload with checklist tracking.** The intake form includes a document upload section with a specific checklist based on the client type. For a new business client, the checklist might include: articles of incorporation, operating agreement, prior year returns, bank statements, and authorization forms. As documents are uploaded, the checklist updates. Outstanding items trigger automated reminders.

**E-signature integration.** Engagement letters, authorization forms, and other documents requiring signatures are presented within the intake flow. The client reviews and signs digitally as part of the same process rather than receiving a separate email with a separate signing link. Signed documents are automatically filed.

**Data routing and system population.** When the intake form is submitted, the data routes to your systems: the client record is created or updated in your CRM, relevant fields populate in your practice management platform, and document files are stored in the appropriate client folder. Routing eliminates manual re-entry.

**Intake status tracking.** A dashboard shows the status of every intake in progress: which clients have started, which have completed, which have outstanding items, and which are overdue. The dashboard gives your team visibility into the intake pipeline without checking each client individually.

## How Intake Forms Are Configured

**Client type mapping.** We identify every client type your firm serves (individual, sole proprietor, partnership, S-corp, C-corp, trust, estate, nonprofit) and map the information required for each. The form logic is built from this mapping so each client type triggers the appropriate fields and document requests.

**Required vs. optional fields.** Fields are classified as required (the form cannot be submitted without them) or optional (helpful but not blocking). Required fields ensure minimum data completeness. Optional fields capture additional context that improves the engagement but does not delay the onboarding.

**Document requirements by engagement.** The document checklist varies by engagement type. A tax preparation engagement requires different documents than a bookkeeping engagement or an advisory engagement. Document requirements are mapped to engagement types and automatically applied based on the client's selected services.

**Integration and routing rules.** Each data field maps to a destination: which CRM field it populates, which practice management field it fills, and which team member receives a notification. Routing rules are configured during setup and tested to ensure data flows correctly into every downstream system.

## Your Questions Answered

**Can we customize the intake form for different service lines?**
Yes. Each service line can have its own intake form or its own section within a shared form. Tax clients, bookkeeping clients, and advisory clients each see only the fields and document requests relevant to their engagement.

**What if a client starts the form but does not finish?**
Partially completed forms are saved automatically. The system sends reminders at intervals you define, and the client can return to the form and continue where they left off without re-entering information.
