# Automated Client Updates for Accounting Firms

Automated client updates for accounting firms solve the information gap that exists between your team doing the work and your clients knowing what is happening. When an engagement moves from one stage to the next, when a deliverable is ready for review, when a filing is submitted, or when a deadline is approaching, clients should know without having to ask. Automated updates deliver that information based on events in your workflow, eliminating the manual effort of composing and sending individual status messages.

## The Information Gap

Clients do not see your internal workflow. They do not know that their tax return moved from preparation to review yesterday. They do not know that their financial statements are waiting for a partner's signature. They do not know that their engagement is on track unless someone tells them.

**The "what's happening?" call.** When clients feel uninformed, they call or email to ask for a status update. Your team stops what they are working on, looks up the client's engagement, composes a response, and sends it. One call takes a few minutes. Multiply that across dozens of clients during busy season, and the interruption cost is significant.

**The perception of inactivity.** Even when your team is working diligently on a client's engagement, silence creates the impression that nothing is happening. Clients who hear nothing assume nothing is being done. This perception erodes trust and sometimes leads to difficult conversations that could have been prevented by a simple status update sent at the right time.

**The inconsistency problem.** Some team members are good about proactively updating clients. Others communicate only when asked. Clients notice the difference, especially clients who have worked with your firm for years and have experienced both levels of communication.

**The volume challenge.** An accounting firm with 200 clients and multiple engagement types generates hundreds of status changes per month. Sending individual updates for each one manually is not realistic. The choice becomes: send no updates and deal with client inquiries, or build a system that sends them automatically.

## Types of Updates the System Delivers

**Engagement status updates.** When an engagement moves between stages (received, in progress, under review, ready for client, completed), the client receives a message explaining what just happened and what comes next. The message is specific to the engagement type and stage.

**Document and deliverable notifications.** When a deliverable is uploaded to the portal, when a document is processed, or when a filing confirmation is received, the client is notified with relevant details and any action items on their end.

**Deadline approach notifications.** As deadlines approach, clients receive timely reminders. For tax deadlines, this might be a series of messages: 30 days, 14 days, and 3 days before the deadline. For quarterly reporting clients, it might be a reminder about upcoming document needs.

**Milestone confirmations.** When significant milestones are reached (return filed, financial statements issued, entity formed), the client receives a confirmation with documentation and next steps. These confirmations serve as both notification and record.

**Periodic progress summaries.** For longer engagements, the system can send weekly or biweekly progress summaries that aggregate recent activity into a single update rather than sending individual messages for every small step.

## How the Update System Works

**Event detection.** The system monitors your practice management platform, CRM, and document management system for events that should trigger a client update. When an engagement status changes, a document is uploaded, or a deadline approaches, the event is captured.

**Update template selection.** Each event type maps to a specific update template. The template includes the message content, personalization fields (client name, engagement details, relevant dates), and any action items or links.

**Channel delivery.** Updates are delivered through the configured channel: email, SMS, or client portal notification. The channel can vary by update type. Routine progress updates might go via email. Time-sensitive deadline reminders might go via SMS and email.

**Delivery logging.** Every update is logged against the client record with the timestamp, content, channel, and delivery status. The log creates a complete record of what each client was told and when, available to any team member who needs context.

## Your Questions Answered

**What systems does this integrate with?**
The update system connects to your practice management platform, CRM, document management system, and client portal. Specific integrations depend on your technology stack and are configured during setup.

**Can clients control how many updates they receive?**
Yes. Communication preferences can be configured per client. Some clients want detailed updates at every stage. Others prefer only milestone notifications. Preference settings determine which updates each client receives and through which channel.

**What if an engagement moves through stages quickly?**
The system can consolidate rapid status changes into a single update rather than sending multiple messages in quick succession. Consolidation rules prevent clients from receiving five messages in one day when the work moves through several stages.

**Do updates go out during business hours only?**
Delivery timing is configurable. Most firms set business hours delivery (for example, 8 AM to 6 PM on weekdays) so clients do not receive messages at unusual times. Urgent notifications, like deadline reminders on the due date, can be configured for immediate delivery regardless of time.
