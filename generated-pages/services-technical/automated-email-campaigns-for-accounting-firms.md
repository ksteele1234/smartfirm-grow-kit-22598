# Automated Email Campaigns for Accounting Firms

Automated email campaigns for accounting firms are pre-built, self-running email series designed for specific purposes in your practice. Each campaign has a defined goal, a target audience, a content sequence, and a trigger that starts it. Once configured, campaigns run independently: when a condition is met (a new client signs up, tax season approaches, a client has not engaged in months), the appropriate campaign activates and delivers the right messages to the right people on the right schedule. Your team does not need to create, send, or track individual emails. The campaigns handle it.

## Campaign Types for Accounting Firms

Different situations call for different campaign structures. Here are the core campaign types that accounting firms typically need:

**Welcome and onboarding campaigns.** Triggered when a new client signs their engagement letter. The campaign introduces the firm, sets expectations for the relationship, provides initial instructions (document submission, portal access, contact information), and guides the client through their first weeks. A typical onboarding campaign runs for two to four weeks with messages spaced a few days apart.

**Seasonal outreach campaigns.** Timed to the accounting calendar. Year-end planning outreach in Q4. Tax preparation reminders in January. Extension deadline alerts in March and April. Mid-year estimated payment reminders. Each seasonal campaign targets the appropriate client segment (individual filers, business clients, advisory clients) with content relevant to the season.

**Retention and check-in campaigns.** Periodic outreach to active clients between service interactions. Quarterly check-ins, annual satisfaction surveys, and educational content that provides value and maintains the relationship. These campaigns run on a recurring schedule, ensuring every client hears from the firm regularly rather than only when the firm needs something from them.

**Re-engagement campaigns.** Triggered when a client or prospect stops engaging with your emails or has not interacted with the firm in a defined period. The campaign acknowledges the absence, provides a reason to re-engage (a valuable resource, a seasonal reminder, an invitation to connect), and attempts to bring the contact back into active communication.

**Referral and review campaigns.** Triggered at appropriate moments in the client relationship (after a successful engagement completion, at an anniversary milestone, after a positive interaction). The campaign asks satisfied clients to leave a Google review or refer a colleague, providing direct links and making the action as easy as possible.

**Prospect nurture campaigns.** Multi-step series for contacts who have expressed interest but have not yet become clients. The campaign delivers educational content, builds credibility, addresses common concerns, and periodically invites the prospect to schedule a conversation. These campaigns can run for weeks or months, maintaining contact until the prospect is ready to commit.

## How Automated Campaigns Are Structured

Each campaign is built with the following technical components:

**Trigger.** The event that starts the campaign. Triggers can be date-based (a calendar date arrives), event-based (a form is submitted, an engagement is signed), or behavior-based (a contact has not opened an email in 90 days). The trigger determines who enters the campaign and when.

**Audience.** The segment of contacts who receive the campaign. Audience rules filter contacts based on attributes (client type, service scope, geographic location) and behaviors (engagement history, past purchases, website visits). Audience rules ensure that only relevant contacts receive the campaign.

**Content sequence.** The emails in the campaign, in order, with defined spacing between them. Each email has a specific purpose within the sequence: the first might introduce the topic, the second provides depth, the third includes a call to action. Content is written for the specific audience and campaign purpose.

**Conditional logic.** Rules that adjust the campaign based on recipient behavior. If a prospect clicks a link about advisory services, the next email focuses on advisory. If a client opens the first email but not the second, the third email adjusts its approach. Conditional logic makes campaigns responsive rather than rigid.

**Exit conditions.** Rules that remove contacts from the campaign when appropriate. If a prospect in a nurture campaign schedules a consultation, they exit the nurture and enter the onboarding campaign. If a re-engagement campaign gets no response after the full sequence, the contact is flagged for manual review.
