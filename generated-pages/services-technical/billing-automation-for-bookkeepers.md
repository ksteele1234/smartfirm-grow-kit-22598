# Billing Automation for Bookkeepers

Billing automation for bookkeepers manages the recurring monthly invoicing cycle that is central to a bookkeeping practice: generating invoices based on monthly service agreements, adjusting for scope changes or additional work, delivering invoices with payment links, sending reminders for unpaid balances, and reconciling payments against client accounts. Bookkeeping revenue is primarily recurring, which means billing should be recurring too. Yet many bookkeepers still create each month's invoices manually, adjusting amounts, adding line items, and sending each invoice individually. Billing automation turns the monthly invoicing cycle into a system that runs with minimal manual input.

## The Recurring Billing Problem

Bookkeeping practices bill on a recurring monthly cycle, which should make billing straightforward. In practice, the recurring nature of the work creates its own billing challenges.

**Monthly invoice creation takes time.** A bookkeeper with 30 clients creates 30 invoices every month. Even if each invoice takes only a few minutes to assemble, the monthly billing cycle consumes meaningful time. The task is repetitive but not fully automatic: each invoice may need adjustments for additional services, scope changes, or credits.

**Scope changes create billing inconsistency.** A client adds payroll processing mid-month. Another requests a special report. A third reduces their service level. Each scope change needs to be reflected in the next invoice. Tracking scope changes across multiple clients and ensuring they appear on the correct invoice is a manual tracking exercise that is easy to get wrong.

**Late invoicing delays payment.** When the bookkeeper is focused on month-end close for 30 clients, invoicing for the prior month gets pushed back. Invoices that go out on the 15th instead of the 1st delay payment by two weeks. Across a full year, late invoicing can shift cash flow by a month or more.

**Collection follow up is inconsistent.** When a client does not pay their monthly invoice, someone needs to follow up. With 30 clients, a few unpaid invoices each month is normal. But tracking who has not paid, sending reminders, and following up takes time that competes with client work. Some overdue invoices get reminders. Others are forgotten until the balance grows.

## What Billing Automation Includes

**Recurring invoice generation.** Each client has a billing profile: the monthly fee, the billing date, the services included, and the payment terms. On the scheduled billing date, the system generates the invoice for each client automatically. The invoice reflects the client's current service agreement without manual assembly.

**Scope change management.** When a client's scope changes (new service added, service removed, one-time project completed), the change is recorded in the billing profile. The next generated invoice automatically reflects the updated scope. One-time charges appear on the next invoice and are not carried forward. Recurring changes persist in future invoices. The billing profile is the single source of truth for what each client is billed.

**Automated invoice delivery.** Generated invoices are delivered to clients automatically via email, client portal, or both. Each invoice includes a direct payment link. Delivery happens on the same day as generation, so there is no delay between creating the invoice and getting it to the client.

**Online payment acceptance.** Clients pay via credit card or ACH/bank transfer through the payment link included in their invoice. Online payment is available around the clock. For clients who prefer automatic payment, recurring payment authorization can be configured so the monthly fee is charged automatically without the client needing to take action each month.

**Automated payment reminders.** When an invoice is unpaid after the due date, the system sends reminders at defined intervals. Reminder sequences can include a friendly first reminder, a firmer second reminder, and a past-due notice. Each reminder includes the outstanding amount and a payment link. Reminders go to every unpaid invoice consistently, not just the ones the bookkeeper remembers to follow up on.

**Receipt and confirmation generation.** When a payment is received, the system generates a receipt and sends it to the client automatically. The payment is recorded against the invoice, and the client's account balance is updated. Receipts provide the documentation that both the bookkeeper and the client need for their records.

**Monthly billing summary.** Automated reports summarize each month's billing activity: invoices generated, invoices paid, payments outstanding, aging balances, and collection rate. The summary gives the bookkeeper a clear view of practice revenue without manually compiling billing data.

## Your Questions Answered

**Can clients be set up for automatic monthly payment?**
Yes. Clients who authorize recurring payment have their monthly fee charged automatically on the billing date. The invoice is generated, the payment is processed, and the receipt is sent without the client needing to take action each month. This is particularly effective for clients with fixed monthly fees.

**How are one-time charges handled?**
One-time charges (additional reports, special projects, setup fees) are added to the client's billing profile as a one-time line item. The charge appears on the next generated invoice and is automatically removed from subsequent months. No manual adjustment is needed after the initial entry.

**What if different clients are on different billing cycles?**
The system supports multiple billing schedules. Most bookkeepers bill all clients on the same date (the 1st of the month), but clients can be configured for different billing dates if needed. Each client's invoice is generated according to their specific billing schedule.

**Can clients see their billing history?**
Yes, if a client portal is configured. Clients can view current invoices, past invoices, payment history, and account balances through the portal. This reduces the number of billing inquiries that reach the bookkeeper.
