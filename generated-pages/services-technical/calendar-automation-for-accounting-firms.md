## Calendar Challenges Specific to Accounting Firms

Accounting firms have scheduling complexities that generic calendar tools do not address well:

**Multiple team members, one client experience.** A client might meet with a partner for advisory discussions, a senior accountant for tax review, and a staff member for document collection. Each person has their own calendar, availability, and commitments. Without automation, coordinating across calendars requires checking multiple schedules manually.

**Seasonal capacity shifts.** During tax season, available meeting time shrinks dramatically as preparation work fills the day. In the off-season, meeting availability opens up for advisory, planning, and business development conversations. Calendar automation adjusts available windows seasonally rather than requiring manual changes.

**Meeting types with different requirements.** An initial prospect meeting might be 30 minutes and available to anyone who visits the website. A tax review meeting might be 60 minutes and available only to existing clients during January through April. An advisory planning session might be 90 minutes and restricted to a specific partner's calendar. Each type has unique duration, availability, and access rules.

**Internal versus external scheduling.** Team meetings, engagement planning sessions, and internal reviews compete for the same calendar time as client meetings. Automation manages both, ensuring that internal commitments are blocked from client booking and that client meetings do not overwhelm the schedule during already-full periods.

## What Calendar Automation Includes

**Multi-calendar synchronization.** The system reads and writes to every team member's calendar, maintaining a unified view of firm-wide availability. When one person's calendar changes, the system updates scheduling availability across all connected booking pages in real time.

**Availability window management.** Configurable rules define when each team member is available for each type of meeting. Rules can be set by day of the week, time of day, date ranges, and exclusions (holidays, planned absences). Multiple availability profiles can be configured: one for tax season, one for the rest of the year.

**Seasonal calendar adjustments.** Pre-configured seasonal profiles change the firm's scheduling behavior at defined dates. When tax season begins, client meeting availability narrows. When it ends, availability expands. Seasonal transitions happen automatically based on the calendar without requiring someone to reconfigure settings manually.

**Round-robin and load-balanced scheduling.** When meetings do not need to be with a specific person, the system can distribute bookings across available team members. This prevents one person from receiving all the bookings while others have open time. Load balancing rules can be configured based on capacity, role, or expertise.

**Buffer time and travel management.** Configurable buffer time between meetings prevents back-to-back scheduling that leaves no time for preparation or transition. If your firm offers in-person meetings at different locations, buffer time can account for travel between appointments.

**Team visibility.** A shared view shows the firm's complete meeting schedule across all team members, making it easy to see who is meeting with which client, when team members are available, and where scheduling gaps or overloads exist.

## Your Questions Answered

**Which calendar platforms do you integrate with?**
We support Google Calendar, Microsoft Outlook/Exchange, and other major calendar platforms. Most accounting firms use one of these, and the integration provides real-time synchronization between the calendar platform and the scheduling automation system.

**How do seasonal adjustments work?**
During setup, we configure seasonal profiles that define meeting availability for different periods. For example, a "tax season" profile might reduce available client meeting hours and extend working blocks. A "post-season" profile might open up more meeting availability. The system switches between profiles automatically at dates you define.

**Can we still schedule meetings manually when needed?**
Yes. Calendar automation manages the self-service booking and automated scheduling, but your team can still schedule meetings manually by adding them directly to the calendar. Manually scheduled meetings are reflected in availability for automated booking, so there is no conflict.

**What happens when a team member is out sick or on vacation?**
Planned absences can be entered into the system in advance, and the availability automatically adjusts. For unplanned absences, the team member's calendar can be updated to block their availability, and the system will stop accepting bookings for them immediately.
