# Client Access Portal for CPAs

A client access portal for CPAs provides 24/7 self-service access to the information clients need from your firm: tax returns, financial statements, engagement letters, invoices, payment history, and engagement status. Clients access what they need on their own schedule without calling or emailing the firm. The portal eliminates the most common client requests that interrupt your team's workday: "Can you resend my tax return?", "What's the status of my engagement?", "Do I have an outstanding balance?" Each of these questions is answered instantly through the portal, freeing your team from information retrieval and status reporting that adds no value.

## The Information Request Burden

CPA firms receive a steady stream of client requests that are not about professional advice. They are about information retrieval: finding a document, checking a status, or confirming a balance. These requests are simple to answer but collectively consume significant team time.

**Document retrieval requests.** "Can you send me a copy of my 2024 tax return?" "I need last quarter's financial statements for a loan application." "Where is the engagement letter I signed?" Each request requires someone to stop what they are doing, locate the document, and send it. The individual request takes a few minutes. Across hundreds of clients per year, document retrieval requests add up to meaningful time.

**Status inquiries.** "Where are we with my return?" "Has my filing been accepted?" "When will my financial statements be ready?" During busy periods, status inquiries compound the time pressure. Each inquiry interrupts production work, and the answer is usually straightforward: the engagement is in a specific stage, progressing normally.

**Billing questions.** "What do I owe?" "Was my payment received?" "Can you resend my last invoice?" Billing questions require looking up the client's account, finding the relevant information, and communicating it. A portal with billing visibility answers these questions without team involvement.

**Off-hours requests.** Clients need information at times when the firm is not staffed: evenings, weekends, and holidays. Without a portal, these requests wait until business hours, creating a queue that the team addresses alongside the day's production work.

## What the Client Access Portal Provides

**Document library with search.** Every document the firm has shared with the client is available in an organized, searchable library. Tax returns by year. Financial statements by period. Engagement letters. Correspondence. Clients find what they need through browsing or searching rather than requesting it from the firm.

**Engagement status visibility.** Clients see the current status of their active engagements: "documents received," "in preparation," "under review," "ready for your review," "filed." Status updates change automatically based on the engagement workflow, giving clients real-time visibility without calling for updates.

**Billing and payment history.** Current invoices, outstanding balances, payment history, and receipts are all accessible in the portal. Clients can see what they owe, confirm past payments, and access invoices for their own records. Online payment can be initiated directly from the portal.

**Secure messaging.** When a client does have a question that requires human attention, they can send a message through the portal. Messages are tied to their client record, visible to the assigned team members, and responded to through the same channel. This is more organized than email and creates a searchable communication history.

**24/7 availability.** The portal is accessible at any time. A client who needs their tax return at 10 PM on a Saturday downloads it from the portal without waiting for Monday morning. Self-service access accommodates clients' schedules without requiring the firm to extend its hours.

**Customizable access levels.** Different clients can see different types of information based on their engagement and the firm's preferences. Some clients may see all engagement status details. Others may see only documents and billing. Access levels are configured per client or per client type.

## Your Questions Answered

**How does this reduce our team's workload?**
Every client request that is answered by the portal is a request that does not reach your team. If your team handles 20 document retrieval requests, 15 status inquiries, and 10 billing questions per week, and the portal handles 80% of those, your team recovers the time previously spent on 36 requests per week. The impact compounds as the client base grows.

**What if clients still call or email instead of using the portal?**
Some clients will prefer direct contact, especially initially. The firm can guide clients to the portal by responding to routine requests with "I've made that document available in your portal" rather than re-sending it by email. Over time, clients learn that the portal is the fastest way to get what they need.

**Can clients access information for multiple entities?**
Yes. Clients with multiple entities (personal tax returns, business returns, trust returns) can access information for all their entities through a single portal login. Documents and engagement status are organized by entity within the client's portal view.

**Is the portal secure enough for sensitive financial data?**
Yes. The portal provides encrypted data storage and transfer, access controls with authentication, and audit logging. The security is significantly stronger than email for sensitive financial information and meets the standards expected for professional accounting services.
