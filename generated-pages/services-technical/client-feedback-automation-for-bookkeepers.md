## Three Types of Client Feedback

Client feedback automation serves three distinct purposes, each with its own mechanics:

**Satisfaction feedback (internal).** Short surveys that measure how clients feel about your service. These are not public. They are for your practice's internal use: identifying areas of improvement, detecting clients who may be at risk of leaving, and understanding what you do well. Satisfaction surveys are sent at defined intervals (quarterly, semi-annually) or after specific engagement milestones.

**Google reviews (external, public).** Requests that ask satisfied clients to leave a review on your Google Business Profile. Google reviews are publicly visible and directly influence how prospects perceive your practice. Review requests are sent after moments of client satisfaction, identified through engagement milestones or positive satisfaction survey responses.

**Testimonials (external, curated).** Requests for longer-form feedback that can be used on your website, in marketing materials, or in proposals. Testimonials provide more detailed social proof than a Google review and can be targeted to highlight specific services or client types. Testimonial requests go to your most satisfied, most engaged clients.

Each type serves a different purpose, but the automation infrastructure is similar: defined triggers, templated requests, follow up for non-responders, and collection management.

## How the System Works for Bookkeeping Practices

**Satisfaction surveys at key moments.** After the first month of service, after the first quarterly review, and at regular intervals thereafter, the system sends a brief satisfaction survey. The survey is short (typically a few questions) and designed to surface both positive feedback and potential concerns. Results feed into your CRM as satisfaction data for each client.

**Positive satisfaction triggers review requests.** When a client responds positively to a satisfaction survey (high rating, positive comments), the system automatically triggers a Google review request. This two-step approach has a higher conversion rate than sending review requests to all clients because it targets the clients who have already confirmed they are satisfied.

**Google review requests with direct links.** Review requests include a one-click link to your Google Business Profile's review form. The message is brief, personalized with the client's name and service context, and arrives shortly after the positive satisfaction response. Follow up messages are sent to clients who do not respond.

**Testimonial requests to top clients.** Clients with the highest satisfaction scores and longest relationship tenure receive periodic testimonial requests. These are more personal than review requests, often asking the client to share their experience in a specific way: how the bookkeeping relationship has helped their business, what the transition to professional bookkeeping meant for them, or what they appreciate most about the service.

**Negative feedback routing.** When a satisfaction survey returns negative or neutral feedback, the system does not send a review request. Instead, it alerts you (or the appropriate team member) so you can address the concern directly. This routing ensures that negative experiences are resolved rather than amplified, and that review requests go only to clients who are genuinely satisfied.

## The Feedback Cycle

For bookkeeping practices, client feedback automation creates a continuous cycle:

**Collect satisfaction data.** Regular surveys give you a real-time pulse on how clients feel about your service. Trends in satisfaction data reveal whether service quality is improving, declining, or stable.

**Act on negative feedback.** Concerns surfaced through surveys are addressed before they become reasons to leave. The automated alert ensures that dissatisfied clients receive attention quickly rather than quietly churning.

**Convert satisfaction to reviews.** Positive feedback triggers review requests that grow your Google profile. The more satisfied clients you have, the more review opportunities the system generates.

**Gather testimonials for marketing.** Your most satisfied clients provide the detailed testimonials that help prospective clients see themselves in your existing client base. Testimonials tailored to specific service types or industries are particularly effective.

**Use feedback to improve.** Aggregate satisfaction data reveals patterns: which aspects of your service clients value most, which cause friction, and how satisfaction changes over the client lifecycle. This data informs service improvements that produce even higher satisfaction, which produces more reviews, which attracts more clients.

## Your Questions Answered

**What if we are worried about what clients will say in a survey?**
Satisfaction surveys are private and for your internal use. They are designed to surface both positive feedback and constructive concerns so you can address issues before they escalate. The system only triggers public review requests for clients who express satisfaction. Negative feedback stays between you and the client.

**How often should bookkeepers survey clients?**
The frequency depends on your relationship cadence. A quarterly or semi-annual survey is typical for ongoing bookkeeping relationships. After onboarding (first month or two), a survey can provide early feedback on the client experience. We determine the right cadence for your practice during setup.

**Will clients get tired of being asked for feedback?**
The frequency and approach are designed to be respectful of clients' time. Surveys are short. Review requests are infrequent (typically once per year or after a major milestone). Testimonial requests go only to your most engaged clients. The system is configured to prevent survey fatigue while still collecting the feedback you need.

**Can we choose which client testimonials to display publicly?**
Yes. Testimonials collected through the system are provided to you for review. You choose which testimonials to feature on your website or in marketing materials. Clients are informed that their feedback may be used for this purpose, and you can request their approval before publishing.
