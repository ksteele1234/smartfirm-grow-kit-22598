# Client Messaging Automation for Bookkeepers

## The Monthly Messaging Burden

Bookkeeping creates a uniquely predictable communication pattern. Unlike tax preparation, where communication peaks seasonally, bookkeeping communication follows a monthly cycle that repeats indefinitely.

**The same messages, every month.** By the 25th of each month, you need to send document reminders to every client. By the 5th of the following month, you need to confirm receipt or follow up on missing items. By the 15th, you need to send reconciliation summaries or close confirmations. These messages are nearly identical from month to month, varying only by client name and specific details.

**The multiplication problem.** With 20 clients, sending 3 messages per month per client means 60 messages. With 40 clients, that is 120 messages. As your client base grows, the messaging workload grows linearly, consuming more time every month for communication that follows the same pattern.

**The timing discipline.** Bookkeeping communication is time-sensitive. Document requests sent too early get ignored. Requests sent too late compress the close timeline. Follow ups sent at the wrong interval are either too pushy or too passive. Maintaining consistent timing across all clients requires a discipline that manual messaging cannot sustain at scale.

**The channel question.** Some clients respond to email. Some respond faster to text messages. Some need a portal notification. Knowing which channel works best for each client, and using it consistently each month, adds another layer of complexity to what should be routine communication.

## Messaging Workflows for the Bookkeeping Cycle

**Month-end document request workflow.** A calendar-triggered sequence that begins before month-end: an initial document request listing what is needed, a mid-cycle reminder for outstanding items, and a deadline-day final notice. Each message includes a specific list of what has been received and what is still needed per client.

**Close confirmation workflow.** When the monthly close is complete, an automated message informs the client: what was reconciled, any items that require attention, and the financial summary or report link. The close confirmation replaces the manual email you currently send after finishing each client's books.

**Payment and invoicing workflow.** When your invoices are generated, the system sends the invoice with payment instructions and a link to pay. If payment is not received by the due date, a reminder follows. If payment remains outstanding, a second reminder follows with different messaging. The sequence runs without your involvement.

**Document receipt confirmation.** When a client submits documents (bank statements, receipts, invoices), an automated confirmation acknowledges receipt and lists what was received. This eliminates the "did you get my documents?" follow up from clients.

**Onboarding messaging workflow.** When a new client signs on, a multi-step messaging workflow introduces them to your process: what to expect, how to submit documents, portal access instructions, and communication preferences. The onboarding workflow runs automatically for every new client.

**Quarterly and annual messaging.** Beyond the monthly cycle, quarterly and annual messages address topics like estimated tax reminders (coordinated with their CPA), year-end document preparation, W-2 and 1099 processing timelines, and annual engagement letter renewal.

## Multi-Channel Delivery

**Email for detailed communication.** Document requests with checklists, reconciliation summaries, and onboarding instructions are best delivered via email where clients can reference the details later.

**SMS for time-sensitive reminders.** Deadline reminders, payment due notices, and brief confirmations work well as text messages. SMS has higher immediate open rates than email and is better suited for messages that need quick attention.

**Channel preferences per client.** Each client can have a preferred channel configured. Some clients prefer all communication via email. Others want text reminders for deadlines and email for everything else. The system respects per-client preferences rather than applying a one-size-fits-all approach.

**Fallback routing.** If a client does not respond to an email document request within a defined window, the system can escalate to SMS. If SMS does not generate a response, the system alerts you to make a direct phone call. Fallback routing ensures that important messages do not get lost in a client's inbox.

## Your Questions Answered

**Can the system handle different schedules for different clients?**
Yes. While most bookkeeping follows a monthly cycle, some clients may have different close dates, document deadlines, or reporting schedules. The system supports per-client scheduling so each client's messaging aligns with their specific timeline.

**What if I need to send a one-off message that is not part of a workflow?**
Automated workflows handle the recurring, predictable messaging. You can always send a manual message to any client outside of the automated workflows. Manual messages are logged alongside automated ones so the complete communication history is visible.

**How does this work with my existing email and phone?**
The messaging system sends from your email address and (for SMS) from a dedicated number associated with your practice. Clients see messages as coming from you. Replies to emails come back to your inbox. The system augments your existing communication tools rather than replacing them.

**Can I adjust messages after the system is set up?**
Yes. Message templates can be edited at any time. If your process changes, document requirements shift, or you want to adjust the tone of a specific message, the templates are updated and all future messages reflect the changes.
