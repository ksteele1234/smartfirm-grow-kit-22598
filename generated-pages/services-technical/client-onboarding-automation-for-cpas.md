# Client Onboarding Automation for CPAs

Client onboarding automation for CPAs orchestrates the complete process of converting a signed engagement letter into an active, properly configured client relationship. The onboarding process involves dozens of individual steps: sending a welcome sequence, collecting intake information, requesting documents, obtaining e-signatures, creating records in your practice management and tax software, assigning team members, provisioning portal access, and triggering the first engagement workflow. When any of these steps are manual, they depend on someone remembering to do them, in the right order, at the right time. Automation handles the orchestration so every new client receives the same complete onboarding experience regardless of how busy your team is.

## The Onboarding Orchestration Problem

Onboarding a new CPA client is not a single task. It is a sequence of interdependent tasks that span multiple systems, involve multiple team members, and take place over days or weeks. The complexity is not in any individual step. The complexity is in making sure every step happens in the right order, at the right time, without anything being missed.

**The checklist dependency.** Most firms manage onboarding with a checklist: a document, spreadsheet, or mental list of things that need to happen for each new client. The checklist works when onboarding volume is low and one person owns the process. It breaks down when multiple clients onboard simultaneously, when different team members handle different steps, or when the person who owns the checklist is on vacation.

**The timing gap.** A client signs the engagement letter on Monday. The welcome email goes out Tuesday because someone was busy Monday afternoon. The intake form is sent Wednesday. The CRM record is created Thursday. Portal access is provisioned the following week. Each delay is small, but the cumulative effect is a new client who experiences a slow, disorganized start to the relationship.

**The inconsistency problem.** When onboarding is manual, the quality of the experience varies by client. Some clients receive a thorough onboarding with every step completed within 24 hours. Others wait days for portal access or never receive the document request because a step was skipped. The firm intends consistency but delivers variability.

**The scaling constraint.** A firm that onboards 5 new clients per month can manage manual onboarding. A firm that onboards 15 or 20 during a busy period cannot. The onboarding process becomes a bottleneck that limits how many new clients the firm can absorb without dropping the ball.

## What the Onboarding System Automates

**Trigger and initiation.** The onboarding workflow begins automatically when a defined trigger occurs: an engagement letter is signed, a CRM status changes to "new client," or an admin marks a prospect as converted. The trigger eliminates the gap between the client saying yes and the onboarding process beginning.

**Welcome sequence.** An immediate welcome email introduces the client to your firm, outlines what to expect during onboarding, and provides the first action items. Follow up messages in the welcome sequence cover additional topics: how to access the portal, what documents you will need, and how to reach your team.

**Intake and information collection.** An automated intake workflow sends the appropriate forms based on client type (individual, business, trust) and service type (tax, advisory, bookkeeping). The system tracks which forms have been completed, sends reminders for outstanding forms, and routes completed data to the appropriate team member and system.

**Document collection and tracking.** Based on the client type and engagement scope, the system sends a specific document request with a clear list of what is needed. As documents are uploaded to the portal or received via email, the system updates the checklist. Outstanding documents trigger follow up reminders at defined intervals.

**System provisioning.** When the intake data is received, the system triggers internal setup: creating the client record in your practice management platform, adding them to your tax software, setting up their billing profile, and provisioning portal access. Provisioning steps can be fully automated (API-based record creation) or semi-automated (task assignments to team members for steps that require manual configuration).

**Team assignment and notification.** The system assigns the client to the appropriate team members based on rules you define: service type, client tier, team capacity, or geographic location. Assigned team members receive a notification with the client's information and their specific responsibilities.

**Engagement workflow activation.** When onboarding is complete (all forms received, documents collected, systems provisioned), the system triggers the first engagement workflow: a tax preparation workflow, a monthly bookkeeping workflow, or an advisory engagement kick-off. The transition from onboarding to active engagement is seamless.

## Your Questions Answered

**Can the system handle different onboarding processes for different service types?**
Yes. Tax-only clients, advisory clients, bookkeeping clients, and multi-service clients each follow a different onboarding path. The system uses the engagement type to determine which onboarding workflow applies, including which forms to send, which documents to request, and which systems to provision.

**What happens if a client is slow to provide information?**
The system sends automated reminders at intervals you define. If the client does not respond after multiple reminders, the system can escalate by notifying a team member to follow up personally, switching to a different communication channel, or adjusting the onboarding timeline.

**What practice management platforms do you integrate with?**
We work with most major practice management platforms including Karbon, Canopy, and others. We design solutions that integrate with your current tools.

For firms looking to upgrade to a modern, cloud-based practice management system, we often recommend [Karbon](https://karbon.referral-factory.com/uEybmnS7) (affiliate link - we may earn a commission) as it's built specifically for accounting and bookkeeping firms. We work with Karbon regularly and find it integrates well with automation workflows, but we design solutions around whatever platform works best for your needs.

**How do we handle clients who transfer from another firm?**
Transfer clients typically require additional onboarding steps: prior year data collection, authorization forms, and predecessor firm communication. The onboarding system can include a "transfer client" path with these additional steps while following the same core automation framework.
