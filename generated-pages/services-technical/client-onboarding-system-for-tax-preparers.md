# Client Onboarding System for Tax Preparers

A client onboarding system for tax preparers manages the process of getting both new clients and returning clients ready for the tax engagement. Tax preparation onboarding is different from general professional services onboarding because it operates in two modes: new client onboarding (collecting baseline information, prior year data, and establishing the relationship) and returning client onboarding (confirming prior year information, capturing changes, and collecting current year documents). Both modes experience seasonal volume spikes that make manual management impractical. A structured onboarding system handles both client types, at scale, during the months when every hour of preparation time matters.

## The Seasonal Onboarding Challenge

Tax preparation onboarding has a structural timing problem: the clients who need onboarding arrive concentrated in a narrow window, during the exact period when your team has the least time to manage manual processes.

**New client onboarding during peak season.** New clients often sign up in January through March, the busiest period of the year. Each new client requires intake forms, prior year data collection, entity classification, and system setup. Doing this manually while simultaneously preparing returns for existing clients means one of the two gets shortchanged.

**Returning client re-engagement.** Returning clients need a lighter onboarding each year: confirmation that their information is current, notification of any changes in their situation (new job, marriage, home purchase, business started), and collection of current year documents. With hundreds of returning clients, even a lightweight re-engagement process adds up to significant volume.

**The prior year data gap.** New clients transferring from another preparer need prior year returns, basis information, carryforward amounts, and depreciation schedules. Collecting this data requires outreach to the client, possibly to the prior preparer, and verification that the information is complete enough to file accurately. This data collection takes time and often involves multiple rounds of follow up.

**Classification decisions at intake.** During onboarding, the preparer needs to classify the client's situation: filing status, entity types, state filing requirements, estimated payment obligations, and complexity level. These classifications determine which workflows the client enters, which team member is assigned, and how much time to allocate. Getting classification right at onboarding prevents downstream complications.

## How the Onboarding System Works

**Dual-path workflows.** The system operates two distinct onboarding paths: one for new clients and one for returning clients. New clients enter the full onboarding flow with intake forms, prior year data collection, engagement letter execution, and system setup. Returning clients enter a streamlined path with information confirmation, change capture, and document collection. Each path is designed for its specific requirements.

**New client onboarding flow.** The new client path includes: welcome communication with what to expect, a comprehensive intake form with entity-specific fields, a prior year data request with a specific list of needed documents, engagement letter delivery and e-signature, system provisioning (practice management, tax software, portal), team assignment based on complexity and entity type, and an initial document request for the current year.

**Returning client re-engagement flow.** The returning client path includes: a pre-season outreach message with last year's information for the client to confirm or update, a change capture form (new dependents, address changes, income changes, life events), a current year document request with a checklist specific to their filing situation, and reactivation of their engagement workflow from the prior year.

**Prior year data collection workflow.** For new clients, a dedicated workflow manages prior year data collection. The system sends a specific request listing every document needed (prior year returns for all entity types, depreciation schedules, basis information, estimated payment history). As documents are received, the checklist updates. Outstanding items trigger automated follow up at defined intervals.

**Classification and routing.** As intake data is collected, the system classifies the client: individual vs. business entity, standard vs. complex, single state vs. multi-state, estimated payment requirements, and advisory eligibility. Classification determines which preparation workflow the client enters after onboarding is complete and which team member is assigned.

**Seasonal capacity management.** The system manages how many new clients can be onboarded during different periods. If the firm has a capacity limit for new clients during peak season, the onboarding system can queue new client inquiries or adjust the onboarding timeline to prevent overloading the team during the busiest weeks.

## Your Questions Answered

**How early should returning client re-engagement begin?**
Most tax practices start re-engagement in late November or December. This timing gives returning clients enough time to confirm their information, report changes, and begin gathering documents before the preparation season starts in January. The system can be configured to start re-engagement on whatever schedule fits your practice.

**Can the system handle clients with multiple entity types?**
Yes. A client who has individual tax returns, an S-corporation, and a rental property can be onboarded with all entity types captured in a single intake process. The system creates the appropriate records and workflows for each entity type while linking them to the same client.

**What practice management platforms do you integrate with?**
We work with most major practice management platforms including Karbon, Canopy, and others. We design solutions that integrate with your current tools.

For firms looking to upgrade to a modern, cloud-based practice management system, we often recommend [Karbon](https://karbon.referral-factory.com/uEybmnS7) (affiliate link - we may earn a commission) as it's built specifically for accounting and bookkeeping firms. We work with Karbon regularly and find it integrates well with automation workflows, but we design solutions around whatever platform works best for your needs.

**What if a new client cannot provide prior year data before the season starts?**
The system can flag these clients for extension filing and adjust their preparation timeline accordingly. The prior year data collection workflow continues independently until all information is received, at which point the preparation workflow activates.
