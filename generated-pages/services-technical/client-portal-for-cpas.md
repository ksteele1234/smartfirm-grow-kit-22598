# Client Portal for CPAs

A client portal for CPAs centralizes every client interaction into a single, branded online platform: document sharing, engagement letter signing, invoice delivery and payment, secure messaging, and engagement status visibility. Instead of managing client interactions across email, file sharing, e-signature platforms, billing systems, and phone calls, the portal brings everything into one place. Clients log in to a single platform that carries your firm's branding and find everything they need: their documents, their invoices, their engagement letters awaiting signature, and a direct communication channel with your team. The portal replaces the fragmented, multi-platform experience that most CPA firms currently provide.

## The Fragmented Client Experience

Most CPA firms interact with clients through multiple disconnected channels. Each channel serves a purpose, but the cumulative effect is a fragmented experience for both the client and the firm.

**Documents go one way.** Tax returns are emailed as attachments or shared through a file sharing link. Financial statements go through a different delivery method. The client has no single place to find all their documents from the firm.

**E-signatures go another way.** Engagement letters are sent through an e-signature platform. The client receives an email from the e-signature service, signs the document, and it returns to the firm. The signed document lives in the e-signature platform, separate from the client's other documents.

**Invoices go yet another way.** Invoices are emailed or mailed separately from documents and communications. The client may receive an invoice email from the billing platform that looks different from the firm's other communications. Payment happens through a separate payment portal or by check.

**Communication is scattered.** Client questions arrive by email, phone, and text. Firm responses go back through whichever channel the client used. There is no organized communication history. When a team member needs to review what was discussed with a client, they search across email threads, call notes, and text messages.

**No unified view.** The client cannot see the status of their engagement, their document history, their billing history, and their communications in one place. Each interaction requires a different platform, a different login, or a different channel.

## What the Client Portal Provides

**Branded client experience.** The portal is configured with your firm's logo, color scheme, and identity. When clients log in, they see your firm's brand, not a generic software interface. The branded experience reinforces your firm's professionalism and creates a consistent impression across every interaction.

**Document sharing and library.** The firm uploads documents to the client's portal space: tax returns, financial statements, engagement letters, correspondence, and any other deliverables. The client has a permanent, organized library of every document the firm has shared. Documents are organized by type, date, and engagement. Clients download what they need without contacting the firm.

**E-signature integration.** Engagement letters, consent forms, and other documents requiring signature are presented directly in the portal. The client reviews and signs within the portal experience rather than being redirected to a separate e-signature service. Signed documents are stored in the client's document library automatically.

**Invoice delivery and payment.** Invoices appear in the portal with online payment options. The client sees their current balance, reviews the invoice details, and pays by credit card or bank transfer within the portal. Payment history and receipts are available in the billing section. Billing is part of the portal experience rather than a separate system.

**Secure messaging.** When the client has a question or the firm needs information, communication happens through the portal's messaging system. Messages are tied to the client record, visible to the assigned team members, and organized chronologically. Secure messaging replaces email for sensitive communications and creates a searchable conversation history.

**Engagement status visibility.** Clients see the current status of their active engagements: what stage the work is in, what the firm is waiting on from the client, and what comes next. Status updates reflect the actual engagement workflow rather than requiring the client to call for an update.

**Document upload.** When the firm needs documents from the client (source documents, receipts, prior year returns), the client uploads them through the portal. Upload areas can be structured with categories and checklists so the client knows exactly what is needed. Uploaded documents are immediately available to the assigned team members.

## Your Questions Answered

**How is this different from just using email and file sharing?**
Email and file sharing handle individual interactions but do not create an organized, persistent client experience. A portal provides a permanent library of documents, a billing center with payment history, integrated e-signatures, secure messaging, and engagement visibility, all under your firm's brand. The portal is the client's single destination for everything related to their relationship with your firm.

**Can the portal integrate with our existing practice management system?**
Most portal platforms integrate with common practice management systems. Integration can include engagement status synchronization, document delivery triggers, and client data synchronization. The specific integration capabilities depend on your practice management platform and the selected portal solution.

**What if some clients prefer email?**
The portal does not eliminate email as a communication option, but it provides a better alternative for most interactions. Many firms transition gradually: delivering documents through the portal while still accepting email inquiries, then encouraging clients to use portal messaging over time. Per-client preferences can be accommodated.

**How do clients get access to the portal?**
New clients receive a portal invitation as part of the onboarding process. The invitation includes login credentials and instructions for first-time access. Existing clients can be migrated in batches with personalized invitations. Most clients activate their accounts within the first week after receiving the invitation.
