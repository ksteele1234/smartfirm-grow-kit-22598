# Document Automation for CPAs

Document automation for CPAs manages the complete lifecycle of documents in your practice: how they are received from clients, how they are organized and classified, where they are stored, how they are made findable when needed, and how long they are retained. A CPA practice handles thousands of documents per year across hundreds of clients. Without automation, managing these documents requires manual filing, manual organization, and manual search when someone needs to find something. Document automation creates a system where documents are organized, stored, and retrievable without constant human management.

## The Document Management Challenge

CPA firms are document-intensive by nature. Every client engagement involves receiving documents, creating documents, delivering documents, and retaining documents. The volume grows every year, and manual management becomes less sustainable as it does.

**The intake chaos.** Client documents arrive through multiple channels: email attachments, portal uploads, physical mail, file sharing links, and sometimes text messages. Each channel deposits documents in a different location. Without a system that consolidates intake, documents from a single client can be scattered across four or five places.

**The organization burden.** Every document needs to be filed in the correct client folder, in the correct year, in the correct category. A W-2 from John Smith for tax year 2025 needs to end up in John Smith's 2025 tax documents folder. When this filing is manual, it is either done immediately (interrupting other work) or deferred (creating a pile that grows faster than it shrinks).

**The findability problem.** A partner needs last year's financial statements for a client meeting in 10 minutes. Where are they? In the document management system? In a shared drive? In someone's email? Filed under the client's name or the engagement name? Without consistent organization, finding a specific document requires searching multiple locations with uncertain results.

**The retention risk.** CPA firms are subject to document retention requirements. Tax returns and supporting documents must be retained for defined periods. Engagement files must be maintained. Some documents must be destroyed after the retention period expires. Without automated retention management, firms either keep everything indefinitely (storage costs and liability) or risk destroying documents too early.

## What Document Automation Includes

**Centralized document intake.** All documents, regardless of how they arrive, are captured into a single system. Email attachments are automatically extracted and filed. Portal uploads are routed to the correct location. Scanned physical mail is digitized and entered. The result is one place where all documents live, regardless of how they were received.

**Automatic classification.** When a document enters the system, it is classified based on document type (W-2, 1099, bank statement, engagement letter), client association, and tax year or engagement period. Classification can use file names, metadata, or content-based rules. Once classified, the document is filed in the correct location automatically.

**Folder structure management.** The system maintains a standardized folder structure for every client: organized by year, by engagement type, and by document category. When a new client is onboarded, the folder structure is created from a template. When a new year begins, the annual folders are created automatically. Consistency across clients means anyone on the team can find documents in the same predictable structure.

**Document search and retrieval.** Beyond folder-based browsing, the system provides search across all documents: by client name, document type, date range, or content keywords. When a partner needs that financial statement for a meeting, the search returns results across the entire document repository rather than requiring knowledge of which folder to look in.

**Retention policy enforcement.** Retention rules are configured based on document type and regulatory requirements. The system tracks retention periods and alerts when documents are approaching the end of their retention window. Destruction can be triggered automatically or flagged for manual review, depending on your firm's risk tolerance.

**Access control.** Document access is managed by role: partners see everything, managers see their assigned clients, staff see only the documents relevant to their assignments. Access control protects client confidentiality and limits exposure to sensitive information.

## Your Questions Answered

**Can this work with our existing document storage platform?**
Yes. Document automation is configured to work with your existing cloud storage, document management system, or practice management platform. The system routes documents into your current storage infrastructure with improved organization and automation rather than requiring you to migrate to a new platform.

**How does the system handle documents that arrive by email?**
Email attachments can be automatically extracted, classified, and filed when they match defined criteria (sender, subject line, attachment type). The system can monitor specific email addresses or inboxes for incoming client documents and process them without manual intervention.

**What about documents that contain sensitive information?**
Document automation includes access controls that limit who can view specific document types or specific client files. Sensitive documents (tax returns, financial statements, identification documents) can be restricted to authorized team members only.

**Can we migrate existing documents into the automated system?**
Yes. Existing documents can be migrated and organized using the same classification and folder structure rules. Migration can be handled in bulk, with the system classifying and filing existing documents based on file names, metadata, and folder context.
