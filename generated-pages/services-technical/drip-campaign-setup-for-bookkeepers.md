# Drip Campaign Setup for Bookkeepers

Drip campaign setup for bookkeepers involves building time-based email series that deliver messages on a consistent schedule over an extended period. Unlike one-time emails or event-triggered messages, drip campaigns are defined by their cadence: they send the next message at a set interval regardless of recipient behavior. This makes drip campaigns ideal for the long-term nurture, education, and relationship maintenance that bookkeeping practices need. A prospect who is not ready to commit today receives weekly or biweekly emails for months until they are. An existing client receives monthly educational content that keeps the practice top of mind between service interactions.

## What Makes Drip Campaigns Different

Drip campaigns are a specific type of automated email. Understanding what distinguishes them from other email automation helps you know when they are the right tool.

**Drip campaigns are time-based.** The defining feature is consistent, scheduled delivery. Message 1 sends on day 0. Message 2 sends on day 7. Message 3 sends on day 14. The schedule runs regardless of whether the recipient opened the previous message or took any action. Drips are about sustained presence over time.

**Event-triggered sequences are behavior-based.** By contrast, triggered sequences respond to specific events: a form submission, a document upload, a payment. They activate when something happens and stop when the goal is achieved. Drips and triggered sequences serve different purposes and are often used together.

**Drip campaigns serve long-term goals.** Because they run over extended periods (weeks, months, or indefinitely), drip campaigns are best suited for goals that require sustained communication: nurturing a prospect who is months away from a decision, maintaining a client relationship between monthly service interactions, or educating a new subscriber about bookkeeping best practices.

## Drip Campaign Types for Bookkeeping Practices

**Prospect nurture drip.** When a potential client first contacts your practice or subscribes to your email list, they enter a nurture drip. Every week or two, they receive an email that provides value: bookkeeping tips for small business owners, common mistakes to avoid, signs that it is time to hire a professional bookkeeper. The drip builds familiarity and trust over time, so when the prospect is ready to hire, your practice is the one they remember.

**New client education drip.** After onboarding, a new client receives a series of emails that educate them on how to work effectively with their bookkeeper: what to expect each month, how to organize receipts, how to use the client portal, when to communicate about unusual transactions. The drip sets expectations and reduces the most common friction points in the early months of the relationship.

**Client value drip.** A monthly or bimonthly email to existing clients that provides ongoing value: seasonal financial reminders, small business financial tips, industry-specific insights, and updates about services the practice offers. The drip keeps the relationship active between service interactions and positions the bookkeeper as a proactive resource rather than a passive service provider.

**Win-back drip.** For contacts who used to be clients or who stopped engaging with your communication, a win-back drip sends a short series of messages designed to re-establish the relationship. These might acknowledge the gap in communication, offer something valuable, and invite the contact to reconnect.

**Referral cultivation drip.** A slow-cadence drip to your most satisfied clients that periodically reminds them that you accept referrals and makes it easy to introduce someone. Rather than asking once and hoping, the drip maintains a gentle, recurring prompt that catches clients at the moment when someone in their network mentions needing a bookkeeper.

## How Drip Campaigns Are Set Up

The technical setup for a drip campaign involves several components:

**Content planning.** Each message in the drip is mapped out in advance: the topic, the goal, and the call to action. For a 12-message prospect nurture drip, this means planning three months of content that progresses from introductory to more specific as the relationship develops.

**Scheduling configuration.** The time interval between messages is set based on the campaign's purpose. Prospect nurture drips might send weekly. Client value drips might send monthly. The schedule is consistent, which is what makes it a drip rather than an ad hoc email approach.

**Audience enrollment.** The rules that determine who enters the drip and when. A prospect nurture drip enrolls new subscribers automatically. A client education drip enrolls new clients when onboarding completes. The enrollment rules ensure the right contacts enter the right drip at the right time.

**Content creation.** The emails themselves are written to be relevant regardless of exactly when the recipient reads them. Because drip emails are time-based rather than event-based, each message needs to stand on its own while contributing to the series as a whole.

**List hygiene rules.** Contacts who become clients are moved from the prospect drip to the client drip. Contacts who unsubscribe are removed. Contacts whose email bounces are cleaned from the list. These rules keep the drip running to the right people over time.

## Your Questions Answered

**How many emails should be in a drip campaign?**
The length depends on the campaign's purpose. A prospect nurture drip might have 12 to 20 messages spanning several months. A new client education drip might have 6 to 8 messages over the first two months. A client value drip can run indefinitely with new content added over time. We determine the right length for each campaign during setup.

**What if a prospect on the drip becomes a client?**
The system moves them from the prospect drip to the appropriate client drip automatically. This transition is configured through enrollment rules that update when a contact's status changes in your CRM. The contact receives the right communication for their current relationship with your practice.

**How is a drip campaign different from a newsletter?**
A newsletter is typically a single email sent to your entire list on a regular schedule. A drip campaign is a defined series of emails that each contact receives in order, starting from when they enroll. A contact who subscribes today starts at message 1 of the drip regardless of when the campaign was created. A newsletter sends the same message to everyone on the same day. Both have value; they serve different purposes.

**Do I need a large email list for drip campaigns to be worthwhile?**
No. Drip campaigns work for lists of any size because they focus on sustained, individual relationships rather than volume. Even a bookkeeper with 50 contacts benefits from a drip that keeps those contacts engaged over time. As your list grows, the drip campaigns scale without additional effort.
