## The Manual Close Procedure Problem

The financial close consists of specific accounting procedures that must be completed in sequence. Each procedure is well-defined and largely repetitive, yet most firms execute them manually for every client every month.

**Recurring journal entries.** Depreciation, amortization, prepaid expense recognition, accrued expenses, and other recurring entries follow the same pattern every month. The amounts may be identical (straight-line depreciation) or calculated from updated data (accrued payroll based on pay dates). Either way, the entry pattern is predictable and repeatable.

**Account reconciliations.** Bank accounts, credit cards, loans, and balance sheet accounts must be reconciled each month. The reconciliation process involves comparing the general ledger balance to the supporting documentation (bank statements, loan statements, amortization schedules) and identifying and resolving discrepancies.

**Trial balance review.** After entries and reconciliations are complete, the trial balance must be reviewed for accuracy: do revenue and expense accounts reflect the period's activity? Are balance sheet accounts consistent with prior months? Are there unexpected balances or unusual fluctuations?

**Variance analysis.** Month-over-month and budget-to-actual comparisons identify significant changes. A 40% increase in utilities expense or a 25% decline in a revenue category requires investigation. Manual variance analysis means reviewing every account for every client looking for meaningful changes.

**Financial statement preparation.** The final statements (balance sheet, income statement, cash flow statement) must be generated, formatted, and reviewed. For firms that produce financial statements for multiple clients, the formatting and generation work is repetitive even though the underlying data is client-specific.

## What Financial Close Automation Handles

**Recurring journal entry automation.** Recurring entries are configured once and posted automatically each month. Fixed-amount entries (standard depreciation, fixed prepaid allocations) post without calculation. Variable entries (accrued payroll, revenue recognition based on activity) calculate from current data sources before posting. All automated entries are logged with the same detail as manual entries for audit trail purposes.

**Reconciliation automation.** The system pulls bank and credit card transaction data and compares it against general ledger entries. Matched transactions are reconciled automatically. Unmatched items are flagged for review. The system identifies common reconciliation differences (outstanding checks, deposits in transit, bank fees not yet recorded) and presents them in a clear format for the accountant to verify.

**Trial balance validation.** Automated validation checks run against the post-close trial balance: balance sheet equation verification, period-over-period comparison for unusual fluctuations, zero balance checks on clearing accounts, and intercompany elimination validation for multi-entity clients. Validations that pass proceed automatically. Validations that fail are flagged for review.

**Variance calculation and flagging.** The system calculates month-over-month and budget-to-actual variances for all accounts and flags those exceeding defined thresholds. Rather than reviewing every account manually, the accountant reviews only the flagged items and investigates the significant variances. Thresholds can be set by account type, dollar amount, or percentage change.

**Financial statement generation.** Once the close procedures are complete and approved, financial statements are generated from templates that apply your firm's formatting standards. Statements include the current period, comparative periods, and any supplementary schedules required by the client. Generation produces formatted, client-ready reports without manual formatting.

## Your Questions Answered

**Does this replace the accountant's review of the close?**
No. Automation handles the procedural and computational steps of the close. The accountant's judgment is still required for investigating flagged variances, resolving reconciliation differences, and reviewing the financial statements for accuracy and completeness. Automation reduces the procedural workload so the accountant can focus on the analytical work.

**How does this handle clients with unusual or complex entries?**
Recurring entries handle the standard, predictable entries. Non-recurring or complex entries (one-time adjustments, unusual transactions, year-end entries) are prepared manually by the accountant and recorded alongside the automated entries. The system accommodates both automated and manual entries within the same close.

**What accounting software does this work with?**
Financial close automation integrates with major cloud accounting platforms including QuickBooks Online, Xero, and others. Integration capability depends on the platform's API access and the specific procedures being automated. We assess your software during setup and design the automation around your platform's capabilities.

**Can we customize variance thresholds for different clients?**
Yes. Some clients may have naturally volatile accounts where a 20% variance is normal. Others may operate in stable environments where a 10% change is noteworthy. Thresholds are configurable per client and per account category so the flagging reflects each client's specific context.
