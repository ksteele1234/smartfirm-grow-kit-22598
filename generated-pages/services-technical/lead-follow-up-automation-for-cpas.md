# Lead Follow Up Automation for CPAs

Lead follow up automation for CPAs ensures that every inquiry receives a fast, consistent response and a structured series of follow up touches until the prospect either engages or indicates they are not interested. The difference between firms that convert a high percentage of their leads and firms that do not is often not the quality of their services or their pricing. It is the speed and consistency of their follow up. A prospect who fills out a contact form and receives a response within five minutes is far more likely to engage than one who waits two days for a reply. A prospect who receives three thoughtful follow ups over two weeks is far more likely to schedule a consultation than one who receives a single response and then nothing.

## Where Follow Up Breaks Down

CPA firms generate leads through multiple channels: website forms, phone calls, referrals, networking events, and content downloads. Each lead represents a potential client relationship. The problem is not generating leads. The problem is what happens after the lead arrives.

**Slow initial response.** The team is in meetings, preparing returns, or focused on client work. A website inquiry sits for hours or days before someone responds. During that delay, the prospect may have contacted two other firms and received faster responses. Speed to first response is one of the most significant factors in lead conversion for professional services.

**One-and-done follow up.** Someone responds to the inquiry, maybe sends an email or makes a call. If the prospect does not respond immediately, there is no second touch. The lead is effectively abandoned after a single attempt. Most prospects need multiple touches before they engage. One follow up is rarely enough.

**Inconsistent process.** Different team members handle inquiries differently. One sends a detailed email. Another makes a phone call. A third sends a brief text. There is no standard follow up process, which means the prospect experience depends entirely on who happens to respond. Inconsistency means some leads receive excellent follow up and others receive minimal attention.

**No tracking or accountability.** Without a system, there is no way to see how many leads were received, how many received follow up, how quickly follow up happened, or how many converted. Leads that were never followed up on are invisible. The firm cannot improve a process it cannot measure.

## What Lead Follow Up Automation Includes

**Immediate automated response.** When a prospect submits a website form, books a consultation, or enters the system through any configured channel, they receive an automated response within minutes. The response acknowledges their inquiry, thanks them for reaching out, sets expectations for next steps, and can include a link to schedule a call or access a relevant resource. The immediate response demonstrates responsiveness and begins the relationship while the prospect's interest is highest.

**Multi-touch follow up sequences.** After the initial response, the prospect enters a structured follow up sequence. Each touch is designed to add value and invite engagement:

- **Touch 1 (same day):** Immediate automated response with scheduling link
- **Touch 2 (day 1-2):** Personal email from an assigned team member introducing themselves
- **Touch 3 (day 3-5):** Educational content relevant to the prospect's expressed interest
- **Touch 4 (day 7-10):** Case example or social proof demonstrating how the firm helps similar clients
- **Touch 5 (day 14):** Direct invitation to schedule a conversation, acknowledging that the prospect may have been busy

Sequence length, timing, and content are customized based on your firm's services and the typical prospect decision timeline.

**Channel selection and escalation.** Follow up sequences can use multiple channels: email, SMS, and internal task creation for phone calls. The sequence can escalate across channels: if the prospect does not open the first two emails, the third touch is sent as a text message. If the prospect opens but does not reply, a task is created for a team member to make a personal call. Channel selection is based on the prospect's responsiveness and preferences.

**Lead source-specific sequences.** Prospects from different sources often need different follow up. A referral from an existing client receives a warmer, relationship-focused sequence that acknowledges the referral source. A website form submission receives an educational, credibility-building sequence. A consultation booking receives a preparation-focused sequence that helps the prospect get ready for the conversation. Source-specific sequences make follow up relevant to how the prospect found your firm.

**Follow up task assignment.** When the automated sequence calls for a personal touch (a phone call, a customized email), the system creates a task assigned to the appropriate team member with the prospect's information and context. The team member receives a notification and a deadline. Task completion is tracked so that personal follow up steps are not skipped.

**Conversion tracking and reporting.** Every lead is tracked from initial inquiry through follow up to outcome (consultation scheduled, proposal sent, client signed, or lost). Reporting shows lead volume by source, response time, follow up completion rate, and conversion rate by source and sequence. The data reveals which lead sources and follow up approaches produce the best results.

## Your Questions Answered

**How fast should our initial response be?**
Faster is consistently better for professional services lead conversion. Automated responses can be delivered within minutes of the inquiry. The automated response buys time for a personal follow up by immediately acknowledging the inquiry and providing useful next steps. The goal is to respond before the prospect contacts a competitor.

**What if a prospect responds during the follow up sequence?**
When a prospect replies to a follow up email, clicks a scheduling link, or takes another engagement action, the automated sequence pauses or adjusts. The system can be configured to stop automated touches once the prospect engages and transition to personal communication. This prevents the awkward situation of receiving an automated email after the prospect has already replied.

**Can different partners or team members be assigned to follow up on different leads?**
Yes. Lead routing rules assign incoming leads to team members based on criteria like service type, geographic area, lead source, or round-robin distribution. Personal follow up tasks are assigned to the designated team member, and their name and contact information can be used in the automated sequence for a personalized experience.

**What happens to leads that do not convert after the follow up sequence?**
Leads that complete the follow up sequence without converting can be moved to a long-term nurture list for periodic check-ins, added to your newsletter audience, or tagged for seasonal re-engagement. Not every lead converts on the first attempt, but maintaining contact keeps your firm top of mind for when the prospect is ready.
