## Why SMS Matters for Accounting Firms

Text messaging is not a replacement for email. It is a complementary channel that serves specific communication needs better than email does.

**Time-sensitive messages need immediate visibility.** An email about tomorrow's filing deadline may sit unread for hours. A text message about the same deadline is typically seen within minutes. For messages where timing matters (deadline reminders, appointment confirmations, document submission urgency), SMS delivers the visibility that email often cannot.

**Short messages fit the SMS format.** Not every communication needs a full email. "Your financial statements are ready for review in your portal" is a complete message in one text. "Reminder: Q4 estimated tax payment due January 15" needs no additional context. SMS is ideal for brief, actionable messages that do not require the length or formatting of an email.

**Clients increasingly expect text communication.** Many clients communicate with other service providers via text. They receive appointment reminders from their doctor, shipping updates from retailers, and account alerts from their bank. Accounting firms that add SMS to their communication mix meet clients in the channel they are already comfortable using.

**Email deliverability is not perfect.** Marketing emails are filtered, sorted into tabs, or blocked by spam filters. Even well-configured email campaigns lose a percentage of delivery to filtering. SMS bypasses email filtering entirely. For critical communications, sending via both email and SMS increases the likelihood that the message is received.

## What SMS Campaign Automation Includes

**SMS platform setup and integration.** The text messaging platform is configured and integrated with your marketing automation and CRM systems. Integration ensures that SMS campaigns are sent to the right contacts based on CRM data and that message history is logged in the contact record alongside email and other communication.

**Compliance configuration.** SMS marketing is subject to regulations (TCPA in the United States, similar regulations elsewhere) that require explicit consent before sending text messages. The compliance framework includes opt-in collection (consent forms, checkbox on intake forms, text-to-subscribe keywords), opt-out processing (automatic unsubscribe handling), and consent record keeping. Every SMS contact must have documented consent before receiving messages.

**Campaign types.** SMS campaigns are configured for different communication purposes:

- **Appointment reminders.** Automated text reminders before scheduled meetings, with confirmation and rescheduling options.
- **Deadline alerts.** Time-sensitive reminders about tax deadlines, document submission dates, and payment due dates.
- **Document follow ups.** Short messages prompting clients to submit outstanding documents or review delivered reports.
- **Service announcements.** Brief announcements about new services, seasonal availability, or important firm updates.
- **Event invitations.** Invitations to webinars, seminars, or client appreciation events with RSVP links.

**Multi-channel workflow coordination.** SMS messages are coordinated with email campaigns so that contacts are not overwhelmed with duplicate messages. A typical workflow sends an email first, then follows up with an SMS only if the email was not opened within a defined window. Or the workflow sends time-sensitive alerts via SMS and detailed information via email. Channel coordination ensures each message goes through the most effective channel.

**Personalization and segmentation.** SMS messages are personalized with the client's name, their assigned team member's name, and relevant details (appointment time, deadline date, document type). Segmentation ensures that messages are relevant: tax deadline reminders go to tax clients, financial statement notifications go to accounting clients.

**Response handling.** When clients reply to text messages, responses are routed to the appropriate team member or captured in the communication log. Two-way messaging supports brief exchanges (confirming an appointment, answering a quick question) within the SMS channel.

## Your Questions Answered

**Is SMS marketing legal for accounting firms?**
Yes, with proper consent. SMS marketing is regulated by the Telephone Consumer Protection Act (TCPA) in the United States. The key requirement is obtaining explicit consent before sending text messages. Our setup includes a compliance framework with opt-in collection, consent documentation, and automatic opt-out processing that meets regulatory requirements.

**How do we collect consent to text clients?**
Consent can be collected through multiple channels: a checkbox on client intake forms, a consent form during onboarding, a text-to-subscribe keyword, or an opt-in request through email. The consent mechanism is documented and stored in the CRM. We configure the opt-in collection as part of setup.

**Will clients find text messages from an accounting firm intrusive?**
When SMS is used appropriately (time-sensitive reminders, brief actionable messages, and communications the client has consented to), it is typically well received. The key is using SMS for messages that benefit from immediate delivery rather than sending promotional content via text. Appointment reminders, deadline alerts, and document follow ups are message types that clients generally appreciate receiving by text.

**How many text messages should we send per month?**
Less is more with SMS. Most firms send two to four text messages per client per month: appointment reminders, an occasional deadline alert, and a document follow up when needed. SMS should be reserved for messages that genuinely benefit from the immediacy of text delivery. High-frequency texting erodes the effectiveness of the channel and risks opt-outs.
