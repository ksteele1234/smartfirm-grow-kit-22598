# Marketing Automation for CPAs with Email Sequences

Marketing automation for CPAs with email sequences replaces the manual, one-off email approach that most firms default to with structured, automated email workflows that run continuously in the background. Instead of composing individual emails when someone remembers to follow up, email sequences deliver the right message at the right time based on where each contact stands in their relationship with your firm. A prospect who downloads a tax planning guide receives a sequence of educational emails that build credibility and invite a conversation. A new client receives a welcome sequence that sets expectations and introduces your services. An existing client receives seasonal sequences that remind them of deadlines and available services. Each sequence runs automatically once it is built.

## Why Most CPA Firms Underuse Email

Email is the most effective marketing channel for professional services firms because it reaches people who have already expressed interest in your firm. Yet most CPA firms use email only for transactional purposes (sending documents, confirming appointments) and occasional newsletters that go out when someone finds time to write one.

**No system for follow up.** A prospect fills out a contact form or calls for information. Someone responds, maybe sends a proposal. If the prospect does not immediately engage, there is no follow up. The lead sits in the CRM or inbox without further contact. Email sequences automate follow up so that every prospect receives a consistent series of touches after their initial inquiry.

**Inconsistent client communication.** Client communication happens reactively: when there is a document to deliver, a deadline to announce, or a question to answer. Proactive communication (educational content, service updates, seasonal reminders) happens sporadically because composing and sending these emails takes time. Sequences make proactive communication automatic.

**One-size-fits-all messaging.** When the firm does send a newsletter or announcement, every contact receives the same message regardless of whether they are a prospect, a new client, or a long-term client. Email sequences deliver different content to different audiences based on their stage, interests, and engagement history.

**No measurement.** When emails are sent manually, there is no tracking of opens, clicks, replies, or conversions. Without measurement, the firm cannot tell which messages resonate and which are ignored. Automated email platforms track engagement metrics for every email in every sequence.

## What Email Sequence Automation Includes

**Welcome sequences for new clients.** When a new client signs the engagement letter, they enter a welcome sequence: a series of emails that introduce the team, explain what to expect, direct them to the client portal, and share useful resources. The welcome sequence runs automatically for every new client, ensuring a consistent onboarding communication experience.

**Lead nurture sequences.** When a prospect enters the system (through a website form, a consultation request, a referral, or a content download), they enter a nurture sequence. Nurture sequences deliver educational content, introduce the firm's services, share relevant insights, and include calls to action (schedule a call, request a proposal). The sequence builds familiarity and credibility over time.

**Seasonal and deadline sequences.** Tax deadlines, estimated payment due dates, year-end planning windows, and other calendar-driven events trigger seasonal sequences. Clients receive timely reminders about upcoming deadlines and available services. Sequences are built once and run every year, with content refreshed as needed.

**Re-engagement sequences.** Contacts who have not opened emails or interacted with the firm in a defined period enter a re-engagement sequence. These emails acknowledge the gap and offer a reason to re-engage: a new service, a useful resource, or an invitation to connect. Contacts who still do not engage can be suppressed or segmented for different treatment.

**Cross-sell and service awareness sequences.** Existing clients who use one service (tax preparation) receive sequences that introduce other services (advisory, bookkeeping, financial planning). Cross-sell sequences are triggered by client attributes (industry, revenue, current services) so that recommendations are relevant rather than generic.

**Referral request sequences.** After a positive interaction (engagement completed, positive feedback, milestone reached), clients enter a referral sequence that thanks them for their trust and invites them to refer colleagues or contacts. Referral sequences are timed to follow moments when client satisfaction is highest.

## Your Questions Answered

**How many email sequences does a typical CPA firm need?**
Most firms start with three to five core sequences: a lead nurture sequence, a new client welcome sequence, a seasonal reminder sequence, and one or two service-specific sequences. Additional sequences can be added over time as the firm identifies more communication opportunities. Starting with the highest-impact sequences and expanding is more effective than trying to build everything at once.

**What if we already send a newsletter?**
Newsletters and email sequences serve different purposes. Newsletters broadcast the same content to your full list on a schedule. Sequences deliver targeted content triggered by individual actions or timing. Both can coexist. The newsletter keeps your full list engaged, while sequences deliver personalized follow up based on each contact's situation.

**How do you handle contacts who should not receive marketing emails?**
Sequence assignment respects preferences and categories. Active clients in the middle of an engagement can be excluded from marketing sequences while still receiving operational sequences (welcome, deadline reminders). Unsubscribed contacts are suppressed from all marketing sequences. Segmentation rules control who receives what.

**Do we need to write all the email content ourselves?**
No. SmartFirm writes the email content for each sequence as part of the setup. Content is written in your firm's voice and reviewed by your team before the sequences go live. Ongoing content updates and new sequences can be written collaboratively or by our team based on your preference.
