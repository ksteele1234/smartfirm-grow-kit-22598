# Meeting Scheduling System for Tax Preparers

A meeting scheduling system for tax preparers manages the full meeting lifecycle for every client interaction across the tax engagement: intake consultations, document review meetings, return preparation discussions, delivery and signing appointments, and planning sessions. Each meeting type has different scheduling requirements, preparation needs, and follow up actions. A meeting scheduling system handles all of these through one coordinated platform rather than leaving scheduling to email exchanges and calendar invitations managed individually.

## Meetings Across the Tax Engagement

Tax preparation involves distinct meeting types at different stages of the engagement. Each type has unique scheduling and communication needs:

**Intake consultations.** The first meeting with a new or prospective client. Typically 30 to 45 minutes. Needs a brief intake form to collect preliminary information before the meeting. Available to both prospects and existing clients. Often the meeting that converts a prospect into a client.

**Document review meetings.** Scheduled after the client submits their tax documents. The preparer reviews the documents, identifies missing items, and asks clarifying questions. Needs preparation materials that include the client's document submission status. Available only during preparation season.

**Return review and signing.** The client reviews the completed return with the preparer, asks questions, and approves for filing. Needs the completed return loaded and accessible during the meeting. Usually shorter than the initial consultation but may run longer for complex returns.

**Year-end planning meetings.** Proactive meetings in Q4 to discuss tax strategies for the coming year. Available to advisory and planning clients. Needs preparation materials that include the client's prior year data and relevant planning scenarios.

**Estimated payment review.** Quarterly or semi-annual check-ins for clients with estimated tax obligations. Needs current financial data and prior quarter payment history. Typically shorter meetings with a focused agenda.

A meeting scheduling system manages all of these types through one platform, with each type configured to its specific requirements.

## What the System Manages

**Meeting type configuration.** Each meeting type is defined with its own duration, availability window, booking page, intake form, preparation materials, and follow up sequence. Clients book the right type of meeting and receive the right communication without the preparer configuring anything per meeting.

**Seasonal scheduling rules.** Meeting types are available during appropriate periods. Intake consultations might be available September through March. Document review meetings are available January through April. Planning meetings open in October. The system manages these seasonal windows automatically.

**Preparation workflow integration.** When a meeting is booked, the system triggers preparation actions: sending the client a reminder of what to bring, pulling relevant client data for the preparer's review, and attaching meeting context to the calendar event. The preparer walks into every meeting with the information they need.

**Capacity management.** The system limits meeting volume based on the preparer's capacity during different periods. During peak tax season, available meeting slots might be restricted to prevent scheduling from consuming preparation time. The capacity rules adjust automatically based on seasonal profiles.

**Multi-channel booking.** Clients can book through the preparer's website, through links embedded in emails, or through links provided by the admin team. All bookings go through the same system regardless of the source, ensuring consistent availability and conflict prevention.

## Your Questions Answered

**Can clients book different meeting types from the same page?**
Yes. The booking page can present all available meeting types and let the client choose the one that fits their need. Alternatively, different meeting types can have their own dedicated booking pages linked from relevant contexts (e.g., the document review booking link embedded in the document submission confirmation).

**How does this handle meetings that need to involve multiple people?**
For meetings that require both the preparer and a specialist or partner, the system can check availability across multiple calendars and only show times when all required attendees are available. Multi-participant scheduling rules are configured during setup.

**What if we need to change meeting availability mid-season?**
Seasonal profiles provide the baseline, but availability can be adjusted at any time. If the preparer needs to block out a day or add additional meeting slots during a specific week, the changes take effect immediately and the booking pages reflect the updated availability.

**Can clients reschedule or cancel through the system?**
Yes. Confirmation messages include links for rescheduling and cancellation. When a client reschedules, the original slot opens back up and the new slot is confirmed. When a client cancels, the slot is released and the associated communication sequence adjusts accordingly.
