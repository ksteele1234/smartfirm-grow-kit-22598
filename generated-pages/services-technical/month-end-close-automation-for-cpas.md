# Month End Close Automation for CPAs

Month end close automation for CPAs manages the workflow that drives the monthly close for client accounting engagements: initiating the close checklist, assigning tasks to team members, tracking reconciliation completion, managing review and approval, generating reports, and delivering the finished package to the client. The monthly close is one of the most operationally intensive processes in a CPA firm that provides client accounting services. It repeats every month for every client, involves multiple team members, and requires coordinated completion of interdependent steps within a defined timeline. Automation manages the workflow so the close runs on schedule without manual orchestration.

## The Close Workflow Challenge

For CPA firms delivering client accounting services, the monthly close is a production process that must run reliably for every client, every month.

**The coordination burden.** A typical monthly close involves the staff accountant completing reconciliations, the senior reviewing the work, the manager approving the financials, and the partner signing off. Each person needs to complete their step before the next can begin. Coordinating this sequence across 30 or 50 clients requires constant attention to who is doing what, for which client, and whether they are on schedule.

**The checklist management problem.** Each client has a close checklist: bank reconciliations, credit card reconciliations, accounts receivable and payable review, payroll reconciliation, intercompany entries, and other items specific to the client. Tracking which checklist items are complete, which are in progress, and which are blocked across the entire client base is a management challenge that grows with every client added.

**The deadline compression.** All clients' monthly closes have roughly the same timeline: books need to be closed within the first two weeks of the following month. This creates a predictable capacity crunch every month where every client's close is competing for the same team members' time during the same window.

**The client delivery gap.** When the close is complete, the client needs to receive their reports. In manual processes, this delivery is often the last step to happen because the team moves on to the next client's close immediately after finishing. Delivery delays erode the client's perception of timeliness even when the close itself was completed on schedule.

## What Close Automation Manages

**Automatic close initiation.** At a defined date each month (or when the prior month's bank statements become available), the system automatically creates the close checklist for every client and assigns the initial tasks to the appropriate team members. Close initiation does not wait for someone to remember to start the process.

**Task assignment and tracking.** Each checklist item is assigned to the responsible team member. As items are completed, the checklist updates in real time. Outstanding items are visible to the team lead, who can see which clients are on track and which are falling behind. Overdue items trigger notifications.

**Dependency management.** The review cannot begin until all reconciliations are complete. The manager cannot approve until the review is finished. The client delivery cannot happen until the approval is received. Dependencies are enforced in the workflow so steps proceed in the correct order and no one is waiting to be told they can start.

**Status dashboard.** A centralized view shows every client's close status: not started, in progress, in review, approved, delivered. The dashboard updates in real time as tasks are completed, giving managers and partners a firm-wide view of close progress without checking with individual team members.

**Automated client delivery.** When the close is approved, the system packages the financial reports and delivers them to the client through the configured channel: email, portal upload, or both. Delivery includes a summary message and any notes from the review. The client receives their reports consistently and promptly after the close is finalized.

**Close performance tracking.** The system tracks close metrics over time: days to close per client, task completion rates, review turnaround time, and delivery timing. This data reveals trends and identifies clients or process steps that consistently run late.

## Your Questions Answered

**Can we have different close checklists for different client types?**
Yes. Each client type (industry, complexity, service level) can have its own checklist template. Simple clients may have 8 to 10 checklist items. Complex clients may have 20 or more. Templates are created during setup and applied automatically.

**What about clients who are always late providing information?**
The system can include pre-close steps that request documents from the client before the close begins. If documents are not received by the target date, the system escalates with reminders and can flag the client as "waiting on client" in the dashboard so the team knows the delay is not internal.

**How does this handle months where a team member is out?**
Tasks can be reassigned manually or automatically based on backup rules. When a primary team member is unavailable, their assignments route to a designated backup, and the workflow continues without disruption.

**Can we track close metrics over time?**
Yes. The system records close timing data for every client every month. Over time, you can see trends: which clients are consistently closed quickly, which take longer, and whether your firm-wide close timeline is improving or needs attention.
