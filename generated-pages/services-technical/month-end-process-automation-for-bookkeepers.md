# Month End Process Automation for Bookkeepers

Month end process automation for bookkeepers systematizes the steps that make up the monthly close for every client: requesting documents, categorizing transactions, reconciling accounts, resolving outstanding items, generating reports, and delivering the finished package to the client. Bookkeepers perform these same steps for every client every month. With 20, 30, or more clients, the monthly close is the single largest block of recurring work in the practice. Process automation structures this work into a repeatable workflow that tracks progress, manages dependencies, and ensures nothing is missed for any client during the close cycle.

## The Monthly Close at Scale

The monthly close is the core of a bookkeeping practice. It is also the process most affected by scale. Every new client adds another complete close cycle to the monthly workload.

**The repetitive sequence.** For each client, the close follows the same general sequence: collect documents and bank data, categorize transactions, reconcile bank accounts, reconcile credit cards, review accounts receivable and payable, post any adjusting entries, generate financial reports, and deliver to the client. This sequence is performed 20, 30, or 40 times per month.

**The tracking challenge.** With multiple clients in various stages of the close simultaneously, knowing where each client stands requires active management. Is the bank reconciliation done for Client A? Did Client B submit their receipts? Is Client C's close ready for delivery? Without a tracking system, this information is held in the bookkeeper's memory, which becomes unreliable as client volume grows.

**The document dependency.** The close cannot be completed without the client's documents. Some clients submit everything by the 5th of the month. Others trickle documents in over two weeks. Some need multiple reminders. Managing document status across the entire client base while simultaneously performing close work for clients who are ready requires parallel attention.

**The delivery inconsistency.** After the close is complete, reports need to reach the client. In manual processes, delivery is sometimes delayed because the bookkeeper moves immediately to the next client's close. Some clients receive reports promptly. Others wait. The quality of the close work is the same, but the delivery experience varies.

## How Process Automation Works for the Monthly Close

**Document collection workflow.** At a defined date each month, the system sends document requests to all clients with a checklist specific to their needs: bank statements (if not on automatic feed), credit card statements, receipts, invoices, and any other monthly documents. The system tracks submissions and sends reminders for outstanding items. When all documents are received, the client's close status changes to "ready to start."

**Close task creation.** When a client is ready to start, the system creates the close tasks in sequence: transaction categorization, bank reconciliation, credit card reconciliation, AR/AP review, adjusting entries, and report generation. Tasks are created from client-specific templates that account for the client's accounts, complexity, and any special requirements.

**Progress tracking.** A dashboard shows every client's close status: waiting on documents, in progress (with task-level detail), in review, ready for delivery, delivered. The dashboard updates as tasks are completed, providing a real-time view of the entire client base without checking each client individually.

**Completion triggers.** When the final close task is marked complete, the system triggers report generation and packages the deliverables for client review. If the close requires a review step (for bookkeepers with team members), the review is initiated automatically before delivery.

**Automated client delivery.** Completed reports are delivered to the client through the configured channel with a standard message that includes a summary of key figures, any items requiring client attention, and a link to schedule a review call if needed. Delivery is consistent in timing and presentation for every client.

**Monthly close metrics.** The system tracks close performance: average days to close per client, document collection timing, and delivery consistency. Over time, this data reveals which clients consistently close quickly, which take longer, and where the bottlenecks are.

## Your Questions Answered

**Can we customize the close process for different clients?**
Yes. Each client can have a customized close template based on their accounts, complexity, and reporting requirements. Simple clients may have a shorter close checklist. Complex clients with multiple entities, payroll, or inventory may have additional steps. Templates are created during setup and applied each month.

**What if a client's close is blocked because of missing information?**
The system can mark specific tasks as "blocked" with a reason. Blocked items are visible on the dashboard, and the system can send targeted requests to the client for the specific information needed. The unblocked tasks can continue while the blocked item waits for resolution.

**How does this handle months where I am behind schedule?**
The dashboard shows which clients are ahead of schedule and which are behind. Priority can be adjusted based on client deadlines and importance. The system does not force a specific order; it tracks progress and ensures visibility so you can make informed decisions about where to focus.

**Can the system handle clients with different close deadlines?**
Yes. While most bookkeeping clients follow a similar monthly cadence, some may have specific close deadlines (lender requirements, board meeting dates). The system supports per-client close deadlines and adjusts the document collection and task timing accordingly.
