# New Client Automation for Bookkeepers

New client automation for bookkeepers streamlines the process of turning a signed engagement into a fully operational bookkeeping relationship. Bookkeeping onboarding has unique requirements that distinguish it from other accounting services: you need access to the client's accounting software, bank feeds must be connected, chart of accounts must be reviewed or configured, historical data may need to be cleaned up or migrated, recurring workflows must be established, and the client needs to understand how and when to submit documents each month. New client automation handles these setup steps through a structured workflow that ensures nothing is missed and the first month-end close is not delayed by incomplete setup.

## Why Bookkeeping Onboarding Is Different

Bookkeeping onboarding is not just about collecting information. It is about establishing the operational infrastructure for a recurring monthly engagement. Unlike a tax engagement where the work is project-based, bookkeeping requires ongoing systems, access, and processes that must be set up correctly before the first month's work can begin.

**Software access and configuration.** You need access to the client's QuickBooks, Xero, or other accounting platform. This might mean receiving login credentials, being added as an accountant user, or migrating the client to a new platform. Each scenario has different steps and potential complications.

**Bank and financial feed connections.** Bank feeds, credit card feeds, and other financial data connections must be established and verified. Feed connections sometimes require client authorization through their financial institution, which adds steps and potential delays.

**Chart of accounts setup.** If the client has an existing chart of accounts, it needs to be reviewed for accuracy and consistency with your standards. If the client is starting fresh, the chart of accounts must be built based on their industry, entity type, and reporting needs. Either scenario requires attention before monthly work can begin.

**Historical data assessment.** New clients often have months or years of bookkeeping that needs review: unreconciled transactions, miscategorized entries, or missing records. Before beginning regular monthly work, you need to assess the historical data and determine what cleanup is required.

**Process and expectation setting.** The client needs to understand the monthly rhythm: when to submit receipts and invoices, how to provide bank access or statements, what questions you will ask each month, and when they can expect reports. Setting these expectations during onboarding prevents confusion and friction during the first few months.

## What the Automation Handles

**Welcome and orientation sequence.** An automated message sequence introduces the new client to your process. Messages cover: what to expect during setup, a timeline for completing onboarding steps, how to submit documents each month, who to contact with questions, and what the first month-end close will look like. The sequence is spaced over the first week or two to avoid overwhelming the client with everything at once.

**Access and authorization workflow.** A step-by-step workflow guides the client through granting the access you need: accounting software user invitation, bank feed authorization, payroll system access, and any other platform connections. Each step includes specific instructions for their platform, and the system tracks which authorizations have been completed and which are outstanding.

**Document collection for setup.** Beyond ongoing monthly documents, initial setup requires specific items: prior financial statements, tax returns, bank statements for the catch-up period, existing payroll information, and current vendor and customer lists. The system sends a targeted document request with a checklist specific to the new client's situation.

**Internal setup task management.** While the client completes their onboarding steps, your internal setup runs in parallel: creating the client record in your practice management system, configuring their chart of accounts, connecting bank feeds from your side, setting up their monthly workflow template, and establishing their billing profile. Internal tasks can be automated (where integrations allow) or managed through automated task assignments to you or your team.

**Onboarding completion and handoff.** When all setup steps are complete (client access granted, documents received, chart of accounts configured, bank feeds connected, historical data assessed), the system marks onboarding as complete and activates the client's recurring monthly workflow. The client receives a confirmation message with a summary of their monthly schedule and what to expect at the first close.

The onboarding workflow can include a software assessment step. If the client's current platform is not one you support, the onboarding process can include guidance for migrating to a supported platform, with migration steps built into the workflow.

The onboarding workflow includes a historical data assessment step. If cleanup is required, the system can trigger a separate cleanup engagement workflow that runs alongside or before the regular monthly bookkeeping workflow begins.
