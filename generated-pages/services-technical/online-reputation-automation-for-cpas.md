## Why Online Reputation Requires Ongoing Management

A CPA firm's online reputation is not a one-time project. It is an ongoing presence that needs active management to stay accurate, current, and positive.

**Reviews stop coming without a system.** A satisfied client rarely writes a review spontaneously. Without a request, even delighted clients do not think to leave feedback. Firms that rely on spontaneous reviews accumulate them slowly and inconsistently. Automated review requests generate a steady flow of new reviews that keeps your profile current and builds volume over time.

**Negative reviews go unanswered.** A negative review on Google is visible to every prospective client who searches for your firm. Left unanswered, it creates the impression that the firm does not care. A professional, timely response demonstrates that the firm takes feedback seriously and handles concerns constructively. Without monitoring, negative reviews can sit unanswered for weeks before someone notices.

**Listings become inaccurate.** Your firm's information appears on Google, industry directories, and business listing sites. Phone numbers change, addresses update, team members join and leave, and hours shift. Without monitoring, outdated information persists across listings, creating confusion for prospective clients and inconsistency that can affect search rankings.

**Reputation develops without your input.** Without active management, your online reputation is shaped entirely by whoever decides to leave a review and whatever information is published on directories. Active reputation management means the firm influences the narrative through consistent review generation, thoughtful responses, and accurate listing maintenance.

## What Online Reputation Automation Includes

**Automated review requests.** After positive client interactions (engagement completed, milestone reached, positive feedback received), the system sends a review request to the client. The request includes a direct link to your Google Business Profile (or other configured platform) and a simple prompt that makes leaving a review easy. Review requests are timed to follow moments when client satisfaction is highest. The cadence is configured to generate a steady flow of new reviews without overwhelming any individual client.

**Review monitoring across platforms.** The system monitors your firm's reviews across Google, industry-specific directories, and other platforms where clients may leave feedback. When a new review is posted, the firm receives an alert with the review content, rating, and platform. Monitoring ensures that no review goes unnoticed, whether positive or negative.

**Review response workflows.** When a new review is detected, the response workflow activates. Positive reviews trigger a thank you response (which can be automated with personalized templates or routed to a team member for a personal reply). Negative reviews trigger an escalation: the firm is alerted immediately, a response template is suggested, and the review is flagged for team attention. Timely, professional responses to every review demonstrate that the firm values client feedback.

**Listing accuracy management.** Your firm's name, address, phone number, website, and hours are monitored across major directories and business listing platforms. When inconsistencies are detected (a directory showing an old phone number, a listing with an outdated address), the system flags the discrepancy for correction. Consistent information across listings improves search visibility and prevents prospective clients from reaching the wrong number or address.

**Review performance reporting.** Automated reports track reputation metrics: total reviews, average rating, review velocity (new reviews per month), platform distribution, sentiment trends, and response time. Reports show whether the review generation system is producing results and whether the overall reputation trend is positive.

**Sentiment tracking.** Beyond star ratings, the system can track the themes and sentiment expressed in review text. Are clients mentioning responsiveness? Tax savings? Communication quality? Sentiment tracking surfaces the themes that clients emphasize, providing insight into what the firm is doing well and where client perception could improve.

## Your Questions Answered

**Which review platforms matter most for CPA firms?**
Google is the most important review platform for local professional services because Google reviews appear directly in search results when prospective clients search for CPAs. Beyond Google, industry-specific directories and your firm's website testimonials page contribute to overall reputation. We configure review requests to direct clients to the platforms that have the most impact on prospective client decisions.

**Is it appropriate to ask clients for reviews?**
Yes. Asking clients for feedback is a standard business practice. The review request is framed as an invitation to share their experience, not a demand. Clients who do not wish to leave a review simply ignore the request. Most clients are willing to share their experience when asked directly, especially after a positive interaction.

**How should we handle negative reviews?**
Respond professionally and promptly. Acknowledge the feedback, express appreciation for bringing the concern to your attention, and offer to discuss the matter offline. Avoid arguing, making excuses, or sharing confidential details in a public response. The response is as much for prospective clients reading the review as it is for the reviewer. A professional response to a negative review can actually strengthen your reputation.

**Can we control which clients receive review requests?**
Yes. Review requests can be filtered based on criteria you define: client type, service type, engagement outcome, or team member assessment. Clients in the middle of a dispute or those you prefer not to request feedback from can be excluded. The goal is to request reviews from clients who have had positive experiences.
