# Payment Automation for CPAs

For CPA firms where billing is often delayed because the team is focused on client work, payment automation ensures that invoicing happens promptly, payment options are convenient, and collection follow up is consistent without adding to the team's administrative workload.

## The CPA Billing Problem

CPA firms have a billing pattern that creates specific cash flow challenges: work is front-loaded during busy periods, invoicing is delayed because the team is focused on production, and collection follow up falls to the bottom of the priority list.

**Delayed invoicing.** During tax season, the team completes return after return. Invoicing happens when someone finds time, which is often weeks after the work is done. The delay between completing work and sending the invoice extends the time between completing work and receiving payment.

**Manual invoice creation.** Each invoice is assembled manually: looking up the engagement terms, calculating the fee, creating the invoice in the billing platform, and sending it to the client. For a firm with hundreds of engagements, manual invoicing is a significant time investment.

**Inconsistent follow up.** When an invoice goes unpaid, someone needs to follow up. During busy periods, follow up is deprioritized. Some overdue invoices get a reminder call. Others sit unpaid for months because no one remembered to follow up. Inconsistent collection directly affects cash flow.

**Payment friction.** Clients who receive an invoice by email and must write a check, address an envelope, and mail it are slower to pay than clients who can click a link and pay online. Payment friction delays collection even when the client is willing to pay promptly.

## What Payment Automation Includes

**Automated invoice generation.** Invoices are generated based on engagement triggers: a milestone is reached, a monthly period closes, a time entry is approved, or a project is completed. The system pulls the client's fee arrangement, calculates the amount, and creates the invoice without manual assembly.

**Invoice delivery with payment links.** Generated invoices are delivered to the client via email (and optionally through the client portal) with a direct link to pay online. The client receives the invoice and can pay immediately without switching to a separate payment platform.

**Online payment acceptance.** Clients can pay via credit card, ACH/bank transfer, or other configured payment methods. Online payment is available 24/7, reducing the dependency on business hours and mail delivery for payment receipt.

**Automated payment reminders.** When an invoice is unpaid after a defined number of days, the system sends a reminder. Reminder sequences can include multiple messages at escalating intervals: a friendly reminder at 7 days, a firmer reminder at 14 days, and a past-due notice at 30 days. Each message includes the outstanding amount and a payment link.

**Payment reconciliation.** When a payment is received, the system matches it to the outstanding invoice and updates the billing record. Payment status is reflected in the client's account, the engagement record, and any reporting dashboards. Manual reconciliation is eliminated for online payments.

**Billing reporting.** Automated reports show outstanding invoices, aging balances, payment trends, and collection rates. Reports are generated on schedule so the firm has ongoing visibility into billing and cash flow performance.

## Your Questions Answered

**What payment methods can clients use?**
Online payment typically supports credit cards (Visa, MasterCard, American Express) and ACH/bank transfers. Specific payment methods depend on the payment processor integrated with your billing system. We configure the options during setup.

**Do we still need to handle payments for clients who prefer to pay by check?**
Yes. Payment automation handles online payments automatically. Check payments can still be received and recorded manually in the billing system. The automated reminders and reporting cover all outstanding invoices regardless of payment method.

**What are the fees for online payment processing?**
Processing fees vary by payment method and processor. Credit card transactions typically incur a percentage-based fee. ACH transactions typically have lower per-transaction fees. We provide fee information for recommended processors during setup so you can make an informed decision.

**Can we set different payment terms for different clients?**
Yes. Payment terms (due upon receipt, net 15, net 30) can be configured per client or per engagement type. Reminder sequences adjust based on the payment terms so reminders are sent at appropriate intervals after the due date.
