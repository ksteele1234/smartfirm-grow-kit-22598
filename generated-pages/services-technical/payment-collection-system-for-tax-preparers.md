# Payment Collection System for Tax Preparers

A payment collection system for tax preparers addresses the billing pattern that is unique to tax preparation: revenue concentrates in a few months, work volume makes timely invoicing difficult, and post-season collection becomes its own project. Tax preparers complete hundreds of returns during a compressed season. Each return represents an invoice that needs to be created, delivered, and collected. Without a system that manages collection across the full engagement lifecycle, from deposit collection before work begins through final payment after filing, revenue leaks through delayed invoicing, missed follow up, and payment friction that discourages prompt payment.

## The Seasonal Collection Challenge

Tax preparation billing is fundamentally different from monthly recurring billing. The work happens in a compressed window, the volume is high, and the team is focused entirely on production during peak periods. Each of these factors creates collection challenges.

**No time to invoice during season.** When a preparer completes 15 returns in a week, creating and sending 15 invoices is an administrative task that competes with preparing 15 more returns. Invoicing gets batched, delayed, or skipped entirely during the busiest weeks. The longer the gap between completing work and sending the invoice, the slower the payment.

**Deposit collection is inconsistent.** Many tax preparers collect deposits or retainers before beginning work. Without a systematic process, deposit collection depends on the person handling intake remembering to collect, the client following through, and someone tracking which deposits were received. During high-volume intake periods, deposits slip through.

**Post-season collection becomes a project.** When tax season ends, the firm often has a stack of unpaid invoices from returns completed weeks or months earlier. Collecting on aged receivables requires dedicated effort: pulling the list, contacting each client, tracking responses, and following up repeatedly. This becomes a post-season project that extends the billing cycle well beyond the work itself.

**Payment friction slows everything down.** When the only payment option is mailing a check, clients delay. The return is filed, the immediate urgency is gone, and the check sits on their to-do list. Convenient payment options (online, credit card, bank transfer) reduce the gap between receiving an invoice and submitting payment.

## What the Payment Collection System Includes

**Deposit collection workflow.** When a new engagement is created (new client signs up, returning client confirms for the current year), the system generates a deposit request with a payment link. The deposit amount is based on the engagement type or the client's prior year fee. Payment status is tracked, and the engagement workflow can be configured to hold until the deposit is received.

**Payment-gated delivery.** For firms that require payment before delivering the final return, the system enforces this policy automatically. When the return is complete and the invoice is generated, the client receives the invoice with a payment link. The return is released through the portal or delivered by the team once payment is confirmed. This removes the awkward manual enforcement of payment-before-delivery policies.

**Automated invoicing at completion.** When a return is marked complete in the practice workflow, the system generates the invoice based on the engagement fee, applies any deposit credit, and delivers the invoice to the client with a payment link. No manual invoice assembly. No delay between completion and billing.

**Split payment tracking.** Some clients pay a deposit up front and the balance at completion. Others pay in installments. The system tracks partial payments against the total engagement fee, applies credits, and shows the remaining balance. Split payment arrangements do not require manual bookkeeping to track what has been paid and what is outstanding.

**Online payment acceptance.** Clients pay via credit card or ACH/bank transfer through a payment link included in every invoice and deposit request. Online payment is available around the clock, and payment is confirmed immediately. The convenience of clicking a link versus writing and mailing a check meaningfully accelerates collection.

**Post-season collection sequences.** After the filing deadline passes, any remaining unpaid invoices enter an automated collection sequence. Reminders are sent at defined intervals with escalating messaging: a friendly reminder, a firmer follow up, and a past-due notice. Each message includes the outstanding amount, a payment link, and a reference to the completed engagement.

**Season collection reporting.** Automated reports show collection performance: total billed, total collected, deposits outstanding, invoices aging, and collection rate by engagement type. Reports give the firm visibility into cash flow performance without manual data compilation.

## Your Questions Answered

**Can we collect deposits before starting work on a return?**
Yes. The deposit collection workflow generates a payment request when a new engagement is created. Payment status is tracked, and the engagement can be configured to hold in the workflow until the deposit is confirmed. This systematizes deposit collection without manual tracking.

**What happens when a client has a balance from a prior year?**
Prior year balances can be surfaced when the current year engagement begins. The system can include the prior balance in the current engagement billing or present it as a separate collection item. Policy for handling prior balances is configured based on your firm's approach.

**Can we require payment before releasing the completed return?**
Yes. Payment-gated delivery holds the final deliverable until payment is confirmed. The client receives the invoice with a payment link, and the return is released once payment clears. This enforces the policy automatically without requiring the team to manually check payment status before each delivery.

**What if different engagement types have different fee structures?**
The system supports different fee structures by engagement type: flat fees for individual returns, tiered fees for business returns, hourly for complex engagements. Invoice generation pulls the fee from the engagement record, so each invoice reflects the correct amount for that specific engagement.
