# Payment Processing for CPAs

Payment processing for CPAs involves configuring the payment infrastructure that connects your billing system to the payment methods your clients actually use: credit cards, ACH/bank transfers, and online payment links. Many CPA firms have invoicing in place but lack the payment processing layer that makes it easy for clients to pay electronically. The result is invoices that are delivered promptly but paid slowly because the payment experience is inconvenient. Payment processing setup connects a payment gateway to your billing workflow so that every invoice includes a payment link, every payment is recorded automatically, and the firm accepts the payment methods that clients prefer.

## Why Payment Processing Matters for Collection Speed

The time between sending an invoice and receiving payment is influenced heavily by how easy it is for the client to pay. Payment processing infrastructure directly affects this.

**Check-based payment is slow.** When a client receives an invoice and the only payment option is mailing a check, the payment timeline includes the client writing the check, mailing it, postal delivery, the firm depositing it, and bank processing. This timeline routinely extends payment by one to three weeks compared to electronic payment.

**No payment link means extra steps.** Some firms accept electronic payment but do not include a payment link in the invoice. The client receives the invoice, then must navigate to a separate payment portal, look up their account, and enter the payment amount. Each additional step reduces the likelihood of immediate payment.

**Limited payment methods limit collection.** A firm that accepts only checks excludes clients who prefer to pay by credit card or bank transfer. A firm that accepts only credit cards misses clients who prefer ACH for lower fees. Offering multiple payment methods accommodates different client preferences and removes payment method as a barrier to prompt payment.

**Manual payment recording creates reconciliation work.** When payments are received outside the billing system (checks deposited at the bank, payments made through a separate portal), someone must manually match each payment to the corresponding invoice. Manual reconciliation is time consuming and prone to errors that create billing confusion.

## What Payment Processing Setup Includes

**Payment gateway selection and integration.** The payment gateway is the service that processes electronic payments. Selection considers processing fees, supported payment methods, integration compatibility with your billing platform, and features like recurring payment support. The gateway is integrated with your billing system so that payments flow directly into your billing records.

**Credit card acceptance.** Clients can pay invoices by credit card (Visa, MasterCard, American Express, and other major cards). Credit card payment is often the fastest collection method because clients can pay immediately from their phone or computer. Card processing includes authorization, settlement, and automatic recording against the invoice.

**ACH/bank transfer acceptance.** Clients can pay by ACH (Automated Clearing House) bank transfer, which typically carries lower processing fees than credit cards. ACH payment requires the client to enter their bank routing and account numbers or authorize the transfer through their bank. ACH settlement takes slightly longer than credit card processing but reduces the per-transaction cost for both the firm and the client.

**Payment links in invoices.** Every invoice includes a direct payment link that takes the client to a secure payment page pre-populated with the invoice amount. The client selects their payment method, confirms the payment, and the transaction is processed. One click from the invoice to the payment page, not a separate login or account lookup.

**Recurring payment configuration.** For clients with recurring engagements (monthly advisory, quarterly reviews, annual retainers), the payment system can be configured to charge the agreed amount automatically on a scheduled basis. Recurring payment eliminates the invoice-and-wait cycle for predictable, recurring fees.

**Payment recording and reconciliation.** When a payment is processed through the gateway, it is automatically recorded against the corresponding invoice in the billing system. The invoice status updates to paid, the client's account balance updates, and the payment appears in financial reports. No manual matching, no reconciliation spreadsheets.

**Security and compliance.** Payment processing requires PCI DSS (Payment Card Industry Data Security Standard) compliance for handling credit card data. The gateway and payment page are configured to meet PCI requirements so that the firm does not store, process, or transmit card data in non-compliant ways. Client payment information is secured throughout the transaction.

**Fee analysis and optimization.** Processing fees vary by payment method, transaction volume, and gateway provider. Fee analysis identifies the cost structure for each payment method and recommends configurations that balance client convenience with processing cost. Some firms pass processing fees to clients for credit card payments while absorbing ACH fees to encourage lower-cost payment methods.

## Your Questions Answered

**What are the processing fees for electronic payments?**
Fees vary by payment method and gateway provider. Credit card processing typically involves a percentage of the transaction amount plus a small per-transaction fee. ACH processing typically carries a lower per-transaction fee. We provide fee comparisons for recommended gateways during setup so you can evaluate the cost structure.

**Can we pass processing fees to clients?**
Many firms add a convenience fee for credit card payments to offset processing costs. The legality and implementation of convenience fees varies by jurisdiction and card network rules. We configure the fee structure based on your preference and applicable rules. Some firms absorb ACH fees (which are lower) and pass credit card fees to encourage bank transfer payment.

**Is our firm responsible for PCI compliance?**
When payment processing is handled through an integrated gateway with a hosted payment page, the gateway handles PCI compliance for card data processing. The firm's responsibility is limited to maintaining secure practices around how payment information is accessed and managed. We configure the integration to minimize PCI scope for your firm.

**Can we accept payments for retainer or trust accounts?**
Payment processing can be configured to direct funds to specific accounts based on the payment type. Retainer or trust account payments can be separated from operating account payments in the payment flow. Configuration depends on your banking setup and the capabilities of the selected gateway.
