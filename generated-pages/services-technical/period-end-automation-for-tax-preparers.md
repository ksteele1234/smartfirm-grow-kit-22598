## Period-End Tasks in a Tax Practice

Tax preparers encounter period-end requirements that differ from general accounting firms because the period-end work has a direct relationship with tax filing obligations.

**Quarterly estimated tax calculations.** Business clients and high-income individuals with estimated tax obligations need quarterly calculations: current quarter income, projected annual income, tax liability calculation, and payment voucher preparation. These calculations recur every quarter and follow the same methodology with updated data.

**Quarterly payroll tax reconciliation.** For clients whose payroll the firm oversees, quarterly reconciliation involves comparing payroll records to tax deposits, preparing Form 941 data, and verifying that withholding amounts match reported wages. Discrepancies need to be identified and resolved before the quarterly filing deadline.

**Sales tax period-end.** Clients with sales tax obligations need monthly or quarterly calculations: total taxable sales, tax collected, tax owed, and filing preparation. For clients with multi-state sales tax obligations, the calculation complexity increases but the pattern remains consistent.

**Interim financial reporting.** Business clients often need monthly or quarterly financial reports for their own management purposes, for lender compliance, or for the tax preparer's use in tax planning. Producing these reports from accurate, closed books requires period-end procedures to be completed first.

**Year-end close.** The annual close requires additional steps beyond the monthly close: year-end adjusting entries, depreciation calculations for the full year, inventory adjustments, deferred revenue recognition, and balance sheet cleanup. The year-end close produces the financial data that feeds directly into the tax return.

## What Period End Automation Handles

**Estimated tax calculation workflow.** Each quarter, the system pulls current financial data for clients with estimated obligations, applies the appropriate tax methodology (annualized income method, prior year safe harbor, or current year projection), calculates the estimated payment, and generates the payment voucher or provides the data for filing. Calculations use the same methodology each quarter with the current period's financial data.

**Quarterly compliance checklist.** A recurring quarterly checklist is created for each client with applicable period-end obligations: estimated taxes, payroll tax filings, sales tax filings, and any other quarterly requirements. The checklist tracks which items are complete and which are outstanding. Approaching deadlines trigger notifications.

**Automated recurring calculations.** Calculations that follow the same formula each period (depreciation, loan amortization, prepaid expense allocation) are automated with the current period's data. The system performs the calculation and posts the result, or queues it for review before posting, depending on the complexity and your preference.

**Year-end close workflow.** A specialized year-end workflow extends the monthly close with additional steps: final depreciation calculations, year-end adjusting entries, inventory adjustments, 1099 preparation data gathering, and W-2 reconciliation. The year-end workflow ensures that all tax-relevant adjustments are made before the books are finalized for tax preparation.

**Tax preparation handoff.** When the year-end close is complete, the system prepares the financial data for tax preparation: trial balance export, supporting schedule compilation, and engagement-specific data packaging. The handoff bridges the gap between accounting and tax preparation so the preparer receives organized, complete data.

## Your Questions Answered

**Can the system handle clients with different fiscal year ends?**
Yes. Period-end automation is configured per client based on their fiscal year. A client with a June 30 fiscal year has different period-end timing than a calendar year client. The system manages each client's period-end schedule independently.

**How does this connect to the tax preparation workflow?**
The year-end close workflow includes a handoff step that packages the financial data in the format needed for tax preparation. When the year-end close is approved, the tax preparation engagement is initiated with the financial data already organized and accessible.

**What if a client's estimated tax methodology changes?**
Calculation methodologies can be updated in the system. If a client switches from the prior year safe harbor method to the annualized income method, the calculation rules are adjusted and all future quarters use the updated methodology.

**Does this work for clients where we only do tax preparation, not bookkeeping?**
For tax-only clients who maintain their own books, period end automation can still manage the data collection and review steps: requesting the client's financial data at period end, tracking receipt, and organizing it for tax preparation. The close procedures themselves would be performed by the client or their bookkeeper, but the data handoff to your tax practice is automated.
