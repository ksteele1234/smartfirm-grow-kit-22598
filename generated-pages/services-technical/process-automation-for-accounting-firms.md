# Process Automation for Accounting Firms

Process automation for accounting firms takes the end-to-end processes that your firm runs every day and converts them from manually coordinated activities into automated systems that handle routing, handoffs, decision points, and exception management. A process is broader than a single workflow. It includes the decisions that determine which path to take, the coordination across departments and roles, the escalation when something goes wrong, and the connection between processes that feed into each other. Process automation manages this full scope so your firm's operations run with less manual coordination and more consistent execution.

## Processes vs. Individual Tasks

An accounting firm's operations consist of interconnected processes, not isolated tasks. Understanding the distinction is important because automating individual tasks without addressing the process they belong to delivers limited results.

**The document receipt process.** When a client uploads a document, the process involves: identifying the document type, routing it to the correct folder, notifying the assigned team member, updating the document checklist for the engagement, and checking whether all required documents have been received. Automating one step (notification) without automating the others (routing, checklist update, completeness check) leaves manual work in between.

**The engagement acceptance process.** When a new engagement is accepted, the process spans: creating records in multiple systems, assigning team members, notifying the client, setting up billing, creating the engagement workflow, and scheduling the first milestone. Each step involves different people and systems. Process automation coordinates the entire sequence.

**The review and approval process.** When work is ready for review, the process includes: notifying the reviewer, providing access to the work product, collecting the reviewer's feedback, routing revisions back to the preparer, re-submitting for review, obtaining final approval, and notifying the next step (client delivery or filing). This process often involves multiple rounds and conditional paths that manual management handles inconsistently.

**The billing and collection process.** From work completion through payment receipt, the billing process involves: time and fee calculation, invoice generation, invoice delivery, payment monitoring, reminder scheduling for overdue invoices, and payment posting. Each step connects to the next, and delays at any point affect cash flow.

## What Process Automation Addresses

**Process mapping and documentation.** Before automation, each process is mapped: inputs, steps, decision points, handoffs, outputs, and exceptions. Mapping reveals the actual process (not the idealized version) and identifies where manual effort is concentrated, where bottlenecks occur, and where errors are most common.

**Decision point automation.** Processes include decision points: does this engagement require partner review? Is this document a W-2 or a 1099? Has the client provided all required information? Automated decision points evaluate conditions and route the process down the correct path without someone manually reviewing and deciding.

**Handoff management.** When one person's work feeds into another's, the handoff must be clean: the first person's work is complete, the second person is notified, and all relevant context is transferred. Process automation manages handoffs with automated notifications, context delivery (here is what was done, here is what is needed), and confirmation that the receiving party has acknowledged the handoff.

**Exception routing.** Not everything follows the standard path. A client's situation is unusually complex. A document cannot be identified. A review reveals a material error. Process automation includes exception handling: when a condition is outside the standard parameters, the system routes it to the appropriate person with context about what triggered the exception and what decision is needed.

**Process monitoring.** Automated processes include monitoring: how long each step takes, where bottlenecks occur, which processes complete on time and which do not. Monitoring data helps identify processes that need adjustment and provides the firm with visibility into operational efficiency.

## Core Processes for Accounting Firms

**Client lifecycle processes.** Prospect inquiry through engagement close: lead capture, qualification, proposal delivery, engagement acceptance, onboarding, service delivery, and offboarding. Each stage has its own sub-process, and the lifecycle process connects them.

**Engagement delivery processes.** The service-specific processes for each engagement type: tax preparation, financial statement compilation, advisory work, audit, and bookkeeping. Each has defined stages, quality checkpoints, and deliverable requirements.

**Administrative processes.** The operational processes that support client work: scheduling, document management, communication, billing, time tracking, and reporting. These processes run alongside every engagement and often span multiple engagements simultaneously.

**Quality and compliance processes.** Peer review, documentation standards, filing verification, and regulatory compliance checks. These processes provide quality assurance and regulatory adherence across the firm's work product.

Accounting firms that implement process automation typically see:
