# Review Collection System for Tax Preparers

A review collection system for tax preparers automates the process of requesting, collecting, and managing Google reviews across your client base. For tax preparers specifically, the review collection challenge is shaped by seasonality: the majority of client interactions are concentrated in a few months, which means the window for collecting reviews is also concentrated. A review collection system captures this seasonal opportunity by automatically requesting reviews at the moments of highest client satisfaction, typically right after a return is delivered and the client has a positive outcome. The system runs during your busiest period without adding to your workload.

## The Seasonal Review Opportunity

Tax preparation creates a unique review collection dynamic because of its seasonal concentration.

**Peak satisfaction moments.** The moment a client receives their completed return, especially when the outcome is favorable (a refund, a lower-than-expected balance, a complex situation resolved), is the highest point of satisfaction in the tax engagement. This is the ideal moment to request a review. But during tax season, the preparer is already working on the next return. The moment passes, and the request never goes out.

**Volume creates opportunity.** During tax season, a preparer might complete dozens or hundreds of returns. Each completed return is a review opportunity. Without a system, maybe two or three clients are asked manually. With a system, every completed return triggers a request. The volume that makes manual requests impossible makes automated collection enormously productive.

**Post-season is too late.** Waiting until after tax season to request reviews means asking months after the service was delivered. Client memory fades. The emotional connection to the experience weakens. Response rates drop. A review collection system captures reviews when the experience is fresh, not months later.

## How the Review Collection System Works

**Delivery-triggered requests.** When a client's completed return is delivered through the portal, emailed, or marked as complete in your system, the review collection system activates. A request goes out within a defined window after delivery, timed to catch the client while the experience is still fresh.

**Outcome-aware messaging.** The request message acknowledges the completed engagement: "Your return has been filed and your documents are ready in your portal. We would appreciate it if you could share your experience with a Google review." The message is specific to the tax engagement, not a generic request.

**Direct Google link.** Every request includes a one-click link to your Google Business Profile's review form. The client does not need to search for your firm or navigate to Google. One click opens the review form with your practice pre-selected.

**Timed follow up.** Clients who do not respond within a defined period receive a follow up. The follow up acknowledges that the client is busy (especially during tax season when they have their own deadlines) and provides the link again. A second follow up may go out after another interval. Clients who complete a review are automatically removed from follow up.

**Post-season sweep.** After filing season ends, a final review request goes to clients who completed their engagement but did not receive a request during the season (due to extensions, late filing, or system enrollment timing). This sweep captures additional reviews from the seasonal work.

**Year-round collection for non-seasonal clients.** Tax preparers who also serve advisory, planning, or bookkeeping clients use the same system on a year-round basis for those engagements. The system handles both seasonal and ongoing review collection with different trigger logic for different service types.

## System Components

**Trigger integration.** The system connects to your tax preparation software, client portal, or workflow system to detect when a return is delivered or an engagement is completed. This trigger is the starting point for the review request.

**Request templates.** Pre-written email and text message templates designed for tax preparation contexts. Templates are professional, brief, and include the direct Google review link. Different templates can be used for different client types (individual, business) and different outcomes.

**Follow up automation.** Multi-step follow up sequences with defined timing and alternative messaging for non-responders. Follow up is automated so it does not consume your time during the busiest period.

**Suppression logic.** Rules that prevent requests from going to clients with negative engagement outcomes, billing disputes, or other indicators that a review request would be inappropriate. Suppression also prevents the same client from receiving multiple requests in a short period if they have multiple engagements.

**Monitoring dashboard.** A view that shows how many requests have been sent, how many reviews have been received, the conversion rate, and the overall status of your Google review profile. The dashboard lets you see the system's performance at a glance.

## Your Questions Answered

**When is the best time to request a review from a tax client?**
The ideal window is shortly after the client receives their completed return, when satisfaction is highest. The system is configured to send requests within a defined period after delivery. Exact timing can be adjusted based on your practice's experience with client responsiveness.

**What if a client had a high tax liability? Should we still request a review?**
The suppression rules can be configured to consider engagement context. If your system tracks outcome indicators, the review request can be suppressed for engagements where the outcome might not produce a positive review. During setup, we discuss which criteria should trigger suppression.

**Can the system handle both email and text message requests?**
Yes. The system supports both channels and can be configured to use one or both based on your preference and the contact information available for each client. Text messages often produce higher response rates for review requests because they are shorter and more immediate.

**What if our Google Business Profile is not set up or needs optimization?**
Part of the implementation includes reviewing and optimizing your Google Business Profile. A complete, accurate, and well-maintained profile is the foundation for review collection. If your profile needs work, we address that before activating the review request system.
