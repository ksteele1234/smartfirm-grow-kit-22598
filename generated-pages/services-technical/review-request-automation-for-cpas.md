## Why CPA Firms Struggle with Review Requests

Asking for reviews is universally acknowledged as important and universally avoided in practice. CPA firms face specific barriers:

**It feels awkward.** CPAs are trained professionals who build relationships on trust and competence. Asking a client to write a review feels transactional, like asking for a favor in a professional relationship that should not require one.

**The timing is hard.** The best moment to request a review is right after a positive experience, but that moment passes quickly. By the time someone remembers to ask, the client has moved on. Manual requests are always either too late or too inconsistent.

**Nobody owns it.** In most CPA firms, review generation is not anyone's defined responsibility. Partners assume someone else is asking. Staff assume it is a partner activity. Nobody asks consistently.

**The process has friction.** Even when someone does ask, the client needs to find the Google Business Profile, figure out how to leave a review, and write something. Each friction point reduces the likelihood that the request converts to an actual review.

## How Review Request Automation Works

The automated system removes each barrier:

**Trigger-based timing.** Requests are sent when a defined event occurs: a tax return is delivered, an advisory deliverable is completed, a client onboarding is finished, or a positive interaction is logged in the CRM. The timing is built into the system rather than depending on someone remembering.

**Direct link delivery.** The review request includes a direct link to your Google Business Profile's review form. One click takes the client directly to the review page with your firm pre-selected. No searching, no navigating, no friction.

**Personalized messaging.** The request message references the specific interaction that triggered it: "Thank you for trusting us with your tax preparation this year" reads differently than a generic "please leave us a review." Personalization comes from CRM data and engagement context.

**Automated follow up.** Clients who do not respond to the first request receive a follow up after a defined interval. The follow up uses different messaging than the original request, approaching from a different angle. Clients who leave a review are automatically removed from the follow up sequence.

**Channel flexibility.** Requests can be delivered via email, text message, or both, depending on the client's communication preferences and what produces the best response rates for your practice.

## The Technical Components

**Trigger configuration.** Triggers are set up based on events in your CRM, practice management system, or workflow automation. When the defined event occurs, the trigger activates the review request sequence for that specific client.

**Request template design.** Email and text message templates are created with personalization fields that pull client name, service type, and engagement context from your CRM. Templates are designed to be professional, brief, and specific to the interaction that prompted the request.

**Direct link generation.** The Google review link is configured to open directly to the review form for your firm's Google Business Profile. This eliminates the navigation steps that cause most review requests to be abandoned.

**Follow up sequence.** A multi-step follow up is configured for clients who do not respond: timing between messages, alternative messaging angles, and maximum follow up attempts. The sequence is designed to be polite and persistent without being pushy.

**Suppression rules.** Rules that prevent inappropriate requests: no review request goes to a client with an unresolved complaint, a billing dispute, or a recent negative interaction. Suppression rules also prevent the same client from receiving requests too frequently.

**Response monitoring.** When a new Google review appears, the system identifies which request triggered it and marks the client as having reviewed. The system can also notify your team so they can respond to the review promptly.

## Your Questions Answered

**Is it appropriate for a CPA firm to ask for reviews automatically?**
Yes. The requests are professional, personalized, and timed to positive interactions. Clients appreciate the simplicity of a direct link and a brief, relevant message. The automation makes the process comfortable for both the firm and the client because it removes the awkwardness of a personal ask while maintaining a professional tone.

**What if a client has a negative experience? Will they still get a review request?**
No. Suppression rules prevent requests from going to clients with unresolved issues, billing disputes, or other indicators of dissatisfaction. The system is configured to request reviews from clients who have had positive interactions, not from every client indiscriminately.

**Can we control how often a client receives review requests?**
Yes. Frequency rules ensure that clients are not asked too often. A typical configuration might allow one request per engagement or one per year, depending on the client relationship and the number of services they use. The frequency is configurable during setup.

**What happens if we get a negative review?**
The review monitoring system notifies your team when any review is posted, including negative ones. We provide guidance on responding to negative reviews professionally. Responding constructively to negative reviews actually builds credibility with potential clients who read the review and your response.
