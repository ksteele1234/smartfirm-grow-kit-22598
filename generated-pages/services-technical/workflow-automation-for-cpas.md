# Workflow Automation for CPAs

Workflow automation for CPAs transforms the sequence of steps involved in delivering client engagements from a manually managed process into a system that advances work automatically. Every engagement type in a CPA practice follows a predictable sequence: intake, preparation, review, delivery, and follow up. Within each stage, there are tasks, assignments, dependencies, and deadlines. Workflow automation defines these sequences as templates, applies them to each client engagement, and manages the progression from stage to stage so work moves forward without someone manually tracking what comes next.

## The Engagement Management Problem

CPA firms manage dozens or hundreds of active engagements simultaneously. Each engagement follows a defined sequence of steps. When those steps are managed manually, the coordination burden grows with every client added.

**The tracking burden.** Someone needs to know where every engagement stands: which are in intake, which are in preparation, which are waiting for client documents, which are in review, which are ready for delivery. Without a system that tracks status automatically, this information lives in someone's memory, in email threads, or in spreadsheets that require manual updates.

**The dependency problem.** Workflow steps have dependencies. The review cannot begin until preparation is complete. The deliverable cannot be sent until the partner signs off. When dependencies are managed mentally, steps occasionally proceed out of order. A deliverable is sent before the review is complete. A preparation begins without confirming that all documents have been received.

**The assignment gap.** When a workflow stage is complete, the next team member needs to know it is their turn. In manual processes, this means someone sends an email or walks down the hall. When that notification does not happen, the engagement sits idle until someone notices.

**The deadline cascade.** When one engagement falls behind, it compresses the timeline for everything that follows. A late start on preparation means a compressed review period. A delayed review means a rush delivery. Without automated deadline management, delays cascade and are difficult to recover from.

## How Workflow Automation Works

**Engagement templates.** Each engagement type (individual tax return, business tax return, quarterly review, advisory engagement, audit) has a workflow template that defines the stages, the tasks within each stage, the team members responsible, the expected durations, and the dependencies. When a new engagement begins, the appropriate template is applied to create a specific workflow instance for that client.

**Stage-based progression.** The workflow moves through defined stages: engagement accepted, documents received, preparation in progress, review pending, review complete, deliverable ready, client notified, engagement closed. Each stage has entry conditions (what must be true before this stage begins) and exit conditions (what must be completed before the next stage begins).

**Automated task assignment.** When a stage begins, tasks are assigned to the responsible team members. The preparer receives their preparation tasks when documents are received. The reviewer receives the review assignment when preparation is marked complete. The partner receives the sign-off request when the review is approved. Assignments are based on rules configured in the template: role-based, name-based, or capacity-based.

**Trigger-based advancement.** The workflow advances based on triggers: a task is completed, a document is uploaded, an approval is given, or a deadline is reached. When the trigger fires, the system automatically initiates the next stage. If the trigger requires a conditional check (did all documents arrive, not just some?), the condition is evaluated before advancing.

**Due date calculation.** Task and stage due dates are calculated based on the engagement timeline. If the filing deadline is April 15, the system calculates backward: delivery by April 10, review complete by April 7, preparation complete by March 31, documents needed by March 15. Due dates adjust if the filing deadline changes (extension filed) or if the client's situation changes the timeline.

**Status visibility.** A dashboard shows every active engagement and its current stage, assigned team member, due dates, and any bottlenecks. The dashboard replaces the need to check in with individual team members about engagement status and provides partners and managers with a real-time view of the firm's production pipeline.

## Your Questions Answered

**Can we have different workflows for different engagement types?**
Yes. Each engagement type has its own template with its own stages, tasks, assignments, and timelines. A tax preparation workflow looks different from a quarterly advisory workflow, which looks different from an audit workflow. Templates are created for each service your firm delivers.

**What happens when an engagement does not follow the standard workflow?**
Workflows can accommodate exceptions. Tasks can be added, skipped, or reassigned within an active workflow without breaking the overall template. The system handles the standard path automatically and provides flexibility for the exceptions that require manual adjustment.

**Can the workflow handle engagements with multiple team members?**
Yes. Different stages can be assigned to different team members, and some tasks within a stage can be assigned to multiple people working in parallel. The workflow tracks who is responsible for what and ensures that all parallel tasks are complete before the workflow advances.
