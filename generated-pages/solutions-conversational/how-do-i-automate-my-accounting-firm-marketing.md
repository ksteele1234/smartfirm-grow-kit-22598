## What Can Actually Be Automated (And What Cannot)

There is a lot of hype around marketing automation. Before you invest in it, you should understand the honest breakdown.

**Fully automatable:**
- Instant response to website inquiries
- Lead follow-up email sequences
- Google review requests after completing client work
- Google Business Profile post scheduling
- Email newsletter delivery on a set cadence
- Lead scoring and pipeline stage tracking
- Appointment scheduling and confirmations

**Partially automatable (needs human input):**
- Content creation (AI can assist, but your expertise and voice need to guide it)
- SEO strategy (the system executes, but someone needs to choose the right keywords and topics)
- Proposal creation (templates help, but each prospect needs personalization)

**Not automatable:**
- Discovery calls with prospects
- Building genuine client relationships
- Making strategic decisions about your firm's direction
- Responding to complex or sensitive prospect questions

The goal of automation is not to remove humans from the process. It is to remove humans from the parts that do not require human judgment, so they have more time for the parts that do.

## The Marketing Automation Stack for Accounting Firms

Here is what a typical automated marketing system looks like for an accounting firm:

**Layer 1: Visibility (runs continuously).**
Your website is optimized for search. Content publishes on a schedule. Your Google Business Profile stays active with regular posts and updated information. None of this requires daily attention once it is set up.

**Layer 2: Capture (triggered by prospect action).**
When someone visits your site, fills out a form, or calls your office, the system captures their information automatically. No manual data entry. No prospect falling through the cracks because someone forgot to log the inquiry.

**Layer 3: Nurture (triggered by time and behavior).**
Prospects who are not ready to hire today enter an automated email sequence. Each message delivers value and keeps your firm top of mind. When a prospect opens multiple emails or revisits your website, the system alerts you that they are warming up.

**Layer 4: Convert (triggered by engagement signals).**
When a prospect shows buying signals (scheduling a call, requesting a proposal, clicking pricing pages), the system prioritizes them and notifies the right team member. The handoff from automated nurture to personal conversation happens at the right moment.

## The "Set It and Forget It" Myth

Marketing automation vendors love the phrase "set it and forget it." That is misleading.

A more accurate description is "set it and check in monthly." The system runs on its own, but it works better when someone reviews the data periodically:

- Which email sequences are getting the best response rates?
- Which search terms are driving the most qualified leads?
- Where are prospects dropping off in the pipeline?
- What content topics are resonating with your audience?
