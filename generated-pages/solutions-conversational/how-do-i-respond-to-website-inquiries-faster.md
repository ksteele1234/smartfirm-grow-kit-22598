## Speed Wins, and You Are Probably Losing

Research shows that businesses that respond to inquiries within one hour are seven times more likely to have a meaningful conversation with the prospect than those that wait longer. Within five minutes is even better.

Now think about your firm's typical response time. Someone fills out a form on your website at 2pm on a Tuesday. You are in back-to-back client meetings until 5pm. You see the notification that evening or the next morning. You respond Wednesday. By then, the prospect has already contacted two other firms, and one of them responded in 10 minutes.

This is not about effort. You are busy doing important work. The problem is that the prospect does not know that. All they see is silence, and silence looks like indifference.

---

## The "In a Meeting" Problem

Accounting firm partners and managers spend most of their day in meetings, on calls, or focused on client deliverables. This is exactly what they should be doing. But it creates a structural problem: the person most qualified to respond to an inquiry is the person least available to do it quickly.

The fix is separating the initial response from the personal follow-up.

**Phase 1: Instant automated response (0 to 2 minutes).** The prospect receives a professional acknowledgment immediately. "Thank you for reaching out to [firm name]. We received your inquiry and a team member will be in touch within [timeframe]." This alone prevents most prospects from moving on.

**Phase 2: Qualification (2 to 30 minutes).** An automated sequence asks a few qualifying questions: What services are you looking for? What is your business type? What is your timeline? This gathers information you need and keeps the prospect engaged.

**Phase 3: Personal follow-up (within 4 to 24 hours).** You or a team member follows up with a personalized response, already armed with qualifying information so the conversation is productive from the first call.

The prospect feels responded to immediately. You get the information you need before the call. And you do not have to interrupt client work.

## What Happens Without a System

Without automated response, your firm's inquiry handling looks something like this:

Form submission comes in. Notification goes to a shared inbox or a partner's email. Partner is busy. Email gets buried under client communications. Someone remembers 24 to 48 hours later and responds. By then, the prospect has either hired someone else or lost confidence in your firm.

Even worse: some inquiries never get a response at all. They sit in an inbox, forgotten. Every one of those is a prospect who actively sought out your firm, raised their hand, and got nothing in return. That is marketing money and brand reputation wasted.

## Your Questions Answered

**Will an automated response feel impersonal to prospects?**
Not if it is written well. A professional, branded acknowledgment that says "we received your inquiry and will follow up personally" is better than silence. Prospects do not expect a human to respond in 30 seconds. They do expect to know their message was received. The automated response bridges the gap until a real person can engage.

**What if inquiries come in after hours or on weekends?**
That is exactly when automated response matters most. A prospect who submits a form on Saturday evening and hears nothing until Monday has two full days to contact other firms. An automated system responds immediately, any time of day, and can include a scheduling link so the prospect books a call during business hours.

**We only get a few inquiries per month. Is this overkill?**
The fewer inquiries you get, the more each one matters. If your firm gets 8 website inquiries a month and your response process loses 3 of them to slow follow-up, that is a significant amount of potential revenue walking away. A system that saves even one or two of those prospects per month pays for itself.

**Can the system handle different inquiry types differently?**
Yes. The system can route inquiries based on the service requested, the prospect's business size, or any other criteria from the contact form. Tax inquiries can go to one team member while advisory inquiries go to another, each with different automated response sequences tailored to that service.
