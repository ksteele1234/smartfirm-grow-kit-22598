## The Four Things You Need to Start

Email marketing has a lot of bells and whistles, but only four things actually matter when you are getting started:

**1. A clean email list.** You already have one. Your client list is your email list. Every current client, every former client who left on good terms, and every prospect who has given you their email address. Do not buy email lists. Purchased lists have low engagement, high spam complaints, and can damage your sender reputation.

**2. An email platform.** This is the software that sends and tracks your emails. There are many options at different price points. The right one depends on your firm size, integration needs, and how sophisticated your automation will be. We help you choose during discovery.

**3. Content worth opening.** This is where most firms stall. They sign up for an email platform and then stare at a blank screen. The solution is a content calendar planned in advance. We cover what to write below.

**4. A consistent schedule.** Monthly is the minimum for staying top of mind. Bi-weekly works well for firms with enough content. Weekly is aggressive but effective if the content is genuinely valuable. The most important thing is picking a cadence and sticking to it. A newsletter that goes out every month for 12 months is more valuable than one that goes out weekly for 6 weeks and then stops.

## What to Write About (The 80/20 Rule)

The biggest fear firms have about email marketing is "what do we write about?" Here is the formula:

**80% educational, 20% promotional.**

Your educational content should answer the questions your clients actually ask you. Tax law changes. Deadline reminders. Financial planning tips. Industry-specific insights. If you hear the same question from three different clients in a month, that is your next email topic.

**Seasonal content calendar for accounting firms:**

- **January:** Year-end wrap-up, tax document checklist, W-2/1099 deadlines
- **March/April:** Last-minute tax tips, extension considerations, estimated tax intro
- **June:** Mid-year tax planning, estimated payment reminder
- **September:** Year-end planning strategies, Q3 estimated payment reminder
- **November/December:** Year-end tax moves, charitable giving strategies, retirement contribution deadlines

The promotional 20% is about your firm's services: new service announcements, case studies, team introductions, or invitations to events. Keep it light. Nobody subscribes to a newsletter to be sold to.

## Common Mistakes That Kill Email Marketing

**Sending without permission.** Adding people to your list who did not opt in is a fast path to spam complaints. Your existing clients have an implied relationship, but prospects and old contacts should actively opt in.

**Over-selling.** Every email being a pitch for your services trains people to ignore your messages. Lead with value. Sell sparingly.

**Inconsistency.** Sending four emails in January and then nothing until June tells subscribers you are not serious. Plan ahead and schedule in advance so the cadence stays steady regardless of how busy you get.

**Ignoring mobile.** Over 60% of emails are opened on mobile devices. If your email template does not render well on a phone, most of your audience is having a poor experience.

**Not segmenting.** Sending the same message to individual tax clients and business advisory clients reduces relevance for both groups. Even basic segmentation (business vs. individual, client vs. prospect) significantly improves engagement.

## Your Questions Answered

**Do we need a dedicated email marketing platform, or can we use Outlook?**
Outlook is fine for individual emails but not for email marketing. Marketing platforms handle list management, template design, scheduling, automated sequences, unsubscribe compliance, and performance tracking. These are essential features that Outlook does not provide.

**How often should we send emails?**
Monthly is the minimum for maintaining awareness. Start there and increase to bi-weekly if you have enough content and see good engagement. Whatever you choose, consistency matters more than frequency. A reliable monthly email outperforms an erratic weekly one.

**What if we do not have anyone who can write content?**
This is one of the most common reasons firms delay starting. A done-for-you approach handles content creation for you. We develop the topics, write the emails, and send them for your review before they go out. Your input is approval, not creation.

**How do we measure whether it is working?**
Open rates and click-through rates are the basic metrics. Industry averages for accounting firms are roughly 20 to 25% open rates. Beyond that, we track which emails generate replies, website visits, and new inquiries. Over time, you can see exactly how email contributes to client retention and new business.
