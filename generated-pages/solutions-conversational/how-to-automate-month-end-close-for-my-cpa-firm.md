## The Multi-Client Close Challenge

CPA firms that provide outsourced accounting or bookkeeping services face a unique version of the month-end close problem: they are closing books for many clients simultaneously, each with different complexity levels, different accounting software, and different document availability.

Here is what makes it difficult:

**Every client is at a different stage.** By the 5th of the month, some clients have all their documents ready while others have not sent their bank statements. Your team cannot close in a linear sequence. They are jumping between clients, picking up whoever is ready and putting down whoever is not.

**The checklist lives in someone's head.** Most firms have an informal close process that experienced team members know by heart: reconcile bank accounts, review credit cards, verify payroll entries, review recurring entries, generate reports. But when a new team member takes over a client, they miss steps because the checklist was never documented.

**Waiting dominates the timeline.** The actual close work for most clients takes a few hours. But the elapsed time from month-end to close completion stretches to two or three weeks because of waiting: waiting for bank statements, waiting for client responses to questions, waiting for a reviewer to check the work.

**Reporting is manual.** After closing the books, generating and delivering financial reports to each client is another round of manual work: exporting data, formatting reports, writing commentary, sending emails.

## The Close Checklist as a System

The foundation of month-end close automation is a standardized checklist that every client goes through, every month, without exception.

A typical CPA firm close checklist includes:

**1. Pre-close preparation.** Verify that all bank feeds are current. Confirm that all client-submitted documents have been received. Flag any outstanding items.

**2. Transaction categorization review.** Review all transactions for the month. Reclassify any auto-categorized transactions that were assigned incorrectly. Address unmatched or uncategorized items.

**3. Bank reconciliation.** Reconcile all bank and credit card accounts. Investigate and resolve any discrepancies.

**4. Accounts receivable and payable review.** Verify that AR and AP are current. Record any adjustments for bad debt, prepaid expenses, or accruals.

**5. Payroll verification.** Confirm that payroll entries match payroll reports. Verify tax withholding and employer contribution entries.

**6. Recurring entries.** Post depreciation, amortization, prepaid expense allocations, and any other recurring journal entries.

**7. Review and approval.** A senior team member or manager reviews the completed close for accuracy and completeness.

**8. Report generation and delivery.** Financial statements are generated, commentary is added, and the package is delivered to the client.

When this checklist is built into a system that tracks each step for each client, your team always knows exactly where every client stands in the close process.

## Automating the Repetitive Steps

Not every close task can be automated, but many of the surrounding steps can:

**Automated document requests.** On the first of each month, the system sends each client a reminder to upload any outstanding documents. Follow-up reminders go out automatically for clients who have not responded.

**Bank feed monitoring.** The system checks whether bank feeds are current for each client and flags any disconnections or delays before the close process begins.

**Checklist progression.** As team members complete each step, the system advances the client through the workflow and notifies the next person in the chain (e.g., moving from "preparation complete" to "review queue").

**Report generation.** Standard financial reports can be generated automatically from the accounting software once the close is complete, reducing the manual export-and-format cycle.

**Client delivery.** Reports are packaged and sent to clients with a template message. Personal commentary can be added where needed, but the formatting and delivery happen without manual effort.

## Your Questions Answered

**How many clients can a team member close per month with automation?**
The specific number depends on client complexity, but firms that implement structured close systems with automation consistently report that each team member can handle more clients than before. The time savings come from eliminating manual document chasing, reducing context switching, and automating report generation. The exact capacity increase depends on your current process and how much of it is currently manual.

**Should we close all clients on the same schedule?**
Not necessarily. Some firms close all clients by a fixed date (e.g., the 15th of the following month). Others stagger closes based on client priority, complexity, or when documents become available. The system can support either approach. Staggering can help distribute workload more evenly across the month.

**What if different clients use different accounting software?**
This is common in outsourced accounting. The close checklist and workflow can be standardized even when the underlying software varies. Some steps may differ slightly depending on the platform, but the overall process, tracking, and reporting work across multiple software environments.

**How do we handle clients who are always late with their documents?**
Automated reminders with escalation handle most cases. For persistently late clients, you may need to have a conversation about deadlines and their impact on your ability to deliver timely financials. Some firms build document delivery deadlines into their engagement agreements so there is a clear, agreed-upon expectation.
