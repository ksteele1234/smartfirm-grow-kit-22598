## Triggered Emails vs. Mass Emails

Most CPAs think "email marketing" means sending a monthly newsletter to everyone on your list. That is one approach, but it is not the most valuable one.

The real power of email automation is triggered messaging. These are emails that fire automatically based on a specific event:

**A new client signs an engagement letter.** The system sends a welcome sequence: what to expect, how to submit documents, key deadlines, and contact information for your team. The client feels taken care of from day one without anyone on your team composing a message.

**A tax return is filed and accepted.** The system sends a confirmation, followed by a review request a few days later, followed by a "what to think about for next year" advisory note two weeks after that.

**A deadline is approaching.** Estimated tax payment reminders, document submission deadlines, year-end planning windows. The system knows the calendar and sends proactive reminders before clients have to ask.

**A client has not been contacted in 90 days.** The system flags inactive client relationships and sends a check-in email, preventing the drift that leads to client churn.

Each of these runs without your involvement. You set up the triggers once, and the system executes every time the event occurs.

## The Advisory Upsell Opportunity

Here is something most CPAs miss: your existing tax compliance clients are your best prospects for advisory services. They already trust you. They already pay you. But many of them do not know what else you offer.

Automated email is the most efficient way to educate clients about services they might benefit from:

**After filing a business return,** send a note about tax planning strategies that could reduce next year's liability. Not a hard sell. A helpful insight that naturally leads to "would you like to discuss this?"

**At the start of Q4,** send year-end planning content to clients with business income. This positions you as proactive and opens the door for advisory conversations.

**After major tax law changes,** send targeted updates to clients who are specifically affected. This demonstrates expertise and creates a natural reason for a conversation.

The system segments your client list by return type, income level, or industry and delivers relevant content to each group. A W-2 employee getting a message about corporate tax planning is irrelevant. A business owner getting year-end planning tips is valuable.

## Your Questions Answered

**Will clients know these emails are automated?**
Not if they are well-written. Automated emails should feel like they came from you or your team. They use your name, your firm's voice, and reference relevant details. The goal is for clients to think "my accountant is really on top of things," not "I am getting a marketing email."

**What about clients who prefer phone calls?**
Automated email does not replace personal communication. It supplements it. Your high-touch clients still get phone calls and meetings. But automated emails handle the communications that do not require a personal touch: deadline reminders, document requests, seasonal tips. This frees up time for the conversations that matter.

**How do we handle clients who unsubscribe?**
Client communication emails (deadline reminders, document requests, engagement-related messages) are transactional and do not require marketing opt-in. Newsletter-style content does. The system manages both types appropriately and keeps your list healthy by honoring unsubscribe requests on marketing emails.

**Can we personalize emails by client type?**
Yes. Segmentation is one of the most important features. Business clients receive different content than individual filers. High-net-worth clients receive different messaging than straightforward returns. The system tags clients based on your criteria and delivers relevant content to each group.
