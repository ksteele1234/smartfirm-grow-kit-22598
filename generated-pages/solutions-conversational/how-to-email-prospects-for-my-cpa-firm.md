# How to Email Prospects for My CPA Firm

Learning how to email prospects effectively is a different skill than emailing existing clients. Clients already trust you. Prospects do not. Every email you send a prospect either builds trust or erodes it, and most CPA firms get this wrong by being either too aggressive or too silent.

## Why Prospect Email Is Different From Client Email

Your existing clients have a relationship with you. They expect to hear from you. They open your emails because they know you and your content is relevant to their situation.

Prospects are strangers. They filled out a form or attended a webinar or downloaded a guide. They are interested but not committed. The rules are different:

**Clients tolerate promotional content.** "We are launching a new advisory service" is relevant to them.

**Prospects ignore promotional content.** "Hire us for advisory services" goes straight to trash. They are not ready for that message yet.

**Clients have context.** They know your firm, your team, and your approach.

**Prospects have questions.** They want to know if you are credible, if you understand their situation, and if you are worth their time.

Prospect email needs to answer those questions before it asks for anything.

## The Anatomy of a Prospect Nurture Sequence

A good CPA prospect nurture sequence typically runs 6 to 10 emails over 4 to 8 weeks. Here is a framework that works:

**Email 1 (Day 0): The value delivery.** Deliver whatever the prospect signed up for (consultation follow-up, guide, checklist) and set expectations. "Over the next few weeks, I will send you a handful of resources relevant to [their situation]. You can unsubscribe at any time."

**Email 2 (Day 3): The credibility builder.** Share a piece of content that demonstrates expertise. A tax planning insight, a common mistake to avoid, or a framework for thinking about their situation. No sell. Pure value.

**Email 3 (Day 7): The social proof.** Share a brief case study or client outcome. "A business owner in a similar situation came to us with [problem] and we helped them [result]." Real examples build trust faster than any claim about your firm's capabilities.

**Email 4 (Day 14): The deeper insight.** Address a common misconception or share something counterintuitive about their situation. This positions you as someone who thinks differently, not just another CPA offering the same services.

**Email 5 (Day 21): The soft ask.** "If any of this resonates with your situation, I am happy to have a no-obligation conversation about what might work for your business." This is the first time you ask for a meeting, three weeks into the relationship.

**Emails 6 through 10 (Weeks 4 through 8): Continued value and periodic check-ins.** Continue delivering useful content with occasional soft invitations to connect. Some prospects convert at email 3. Others take months. The sequence keeps you present without being pushy.

## What Not to Do

**Do not buy email lists.** Emailing people who did not ask to hear from you is spam. It violates CAN-SPAM regulations, damages your sender reputation, and does not work. Your prospect list should come from website inquiries, content downloads, webinar attendees, and referrals.

**Do not email daily.** For prospects, one to two emails per week is the maximum during the active nurture phase. After the initial sequence ends, move to a monthly newsletter cadence. More than that, and you become noise.

**Do not lead with your services.** The first several emails should not mention what your firm offers. They should address what the prospect is struggling with. Service information comes later, after trust is established.

**Do not use generic templates.** "Dear [First Name], Are you looking for a CPA?" is immediately recognizable as a mass email. Each message should feel like it was written for someone in the prospect's specific situation, even if it is automated.

## Your Questions Answered

**Is emailing prospects legal?**
Yes, as long as you follow CAN-SPAM regulations. The basics: only email people who have opted in (filled out a form, requested information, attended an event), include a clear unsubscribe option in every email, and honor unsubscribe requests within 10 business days. We set up all workflows to be fully compliant.

**How do we build a prospect list if we are just starting out?**
Start with your website contact form and add lead magnets: downloadable guides, checklists, or calculators that prospects exchange their email for. "Year-End Tax Planning Checklist for Small Business Owners" is a simple example that attracts the right audience. Every download is a new prospect in your nurture sequence.

**What open rates should we expect from prospect emails?**
Prospect emails typically get lower open rates than client emails because the relationship is newer. 15 to 20% is a reasonable benchmark. More important than open rate is engagement over the full sequence. If 5% of prospects book a discovery call by the end of the sequence, that is a strong result for most CPA firms.

**Can we send different sequences to different types of prospects?**
Absolutely. A small business owner considering advisory services should receive different content than an individual looking for a tax preparer. Segmentation by prospect type allows you to tailor messaging, examples, and calls to action to each audience, which significantly improves conversion.
