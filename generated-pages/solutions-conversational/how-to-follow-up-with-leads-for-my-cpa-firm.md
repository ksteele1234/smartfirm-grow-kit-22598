## The Follow-Up Gap Is Costing You Clients

Here is a number that should bother you: only about 2% of sales happen on the first point of contact. The other 98% require continued communication, follow-up, and outreach before the prospect is ready to move forward.

Now think about how your CPA firm handles inquiries. Someone fills out your contact form. Maybe you call them back that day, maybe the next day. You have a conversation. They say "let me think about it." And then what?

For most CPAs, the answer is nothing. You get busy with client work, the prospect's name sinks in your inbox, and three months later they hired someone else. Not because your firm was not the best option. Because you did not follow up.

## Why CPA Follow-Up Is Different From Other Industries

Advisory services have a longer sales cycle than most professional services. When someone needs a tax return filed, the decision is quick. When someone is considering switching their accounting firm, adding advisory services, or engaging a CPA for strategic tax planning, the decision takes weeks or months.

During that decision period, the prospect is:

**Evaluating multiple firms.** They are not waiting for you. They are talking to other CPAs, reading content, and comparing.

**Waiting for a trigger.** Sometimes prospects are not ready now but will be after quarter-end, after a business milestone, or after their current engagement ends. If you are not in front of them at that moment, you lose.

**Looking for reassurance.** Changing accountants feels risky. Every touchpoint that demonstrates your expertise and professionalism reduces that perceived risk.

An automated follow-up sequence keeps you present during this entire decision window without requiring you to manually track each prospect.

## What Good Follow-Up Looks Like for a CPA

Follow-up is not just "checking in." That adds no value and makes you look desperate. Effective CPA follow-up adds value with every touch:

**After the first call:** A personalized summary of what you discussed, with a relevant resource (article, checklist, or guide) related to their specific situation.

**One week later:** A piece of content that addresses a common concern for prospects in their situation. If they mentioned concerns about switching firms, send something about how transitions work.

**Two weeks later:** A brief update or insight relevant to their industry. Tax law changes, deadline reminders, or market observations that show you are paying attention.

**One month later:** A check-in that references their specific timeline or trigger event. "You mentioned wanting to have something in place before Q3. Wanted to make sure we connected before then."

Each message is an opportunity to demonstrate expertise and stay top of mind. The system sends them automatically, triggered by where the prospect is in their decision process.

## Your Questions Answered

**We do not get that many leads. Is this worth setting up?**
That actually makes it more important. If you only get 5 to 10 inquiries a month, every one matters. Losing even one prospect to poor follow-up is a significant percentage of your potential growth. A system that converts 2 more leads per month into clients can meaningfully change your revenue.

**Will automated emails feel impersonal?**
Only if they are generic. The system sends personalized messages based on what the prospect told you, their industry, and their stage in the decision process. Done well, automated follow-up feels more attentive than most manual approaches because it is consistent and timely.

**We use Outlook for everything. Do we need a CRM?**
Outlook is not built to track prospect stages, trigger follow-up sequences, or report on pipeline activity. A CRM does not have to be complex. Even a simple system that tracks who inquired, when you last contacted them, and what their status is will prevent leads from falling through the cracks.

**What about following up during busy season?**
This is exactly when automated follow-up matters most. During busy season, you have zero bandwidth to manually track prospects. The system runs on its own, keeping leads warm while you focus on filing deadlines. Many firms find that their best conversion months are during or right after busy season, when the follow-up system has been nurturing prospects for weeks.
