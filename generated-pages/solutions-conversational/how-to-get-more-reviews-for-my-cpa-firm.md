## Why CPAs Struggle With Reviews More Than Other Professionals

Here is the uncomfortable truth: when someone searches "CPA reviews," Google often returns results about AICPA peer reviews, not client testimonials. Your potential clients are already confused before they find you.

On top of that, the nature of CPA work creates a review problem. Tax returns get filed and clients move on. Advisory engagements are ongoing but rarely have a clear "win" moment where asking for a review feels natural. And during busy season, when you are talking to the most clients, you have the least bandwidth to think about marketing.

Research shows that 90% of consumers check online reviews before engaging with a business. Your competitors who have figured out review generation are capturing those prospects while you rely on referrals alone.

## The Timing Problem (And How to Solve It)

The best time to ask a CPA client for a review is right after their return is accepted. They feel relief, they feel taken care of, and they are grateful. Miss that window and the motivation fades fast.

But you cannot personally remember to follow up with every client at exactly the right moment. Especially not during the weeks when you are filing dozens of returns.

SmartFirm builds an automated system that sends the right message at the right time. After a return is filed, after an advisory milestone is hit, after a positive interaction with your office. The system handles the timing so you never miss a window.

## What Makes Review Automation Different for CPAs

Your review strategy needs to account for things that a generic marketing tool does not understand:

**Busy season awareness.** Review requests should ramp up when you have the most client touchpoints (February through April) and adjust during quieter months.

**Compliance sensitivity.** CPAs operate under state board regulations. Your review requests need to be professional and appropriate, not the kind of aggressive "LEAVE US 5 STARS" messaging that works for restaurants.

**Client segmentation.** Your smaller clients with straightforward returns are often your most loyal and most likely to leave reviews. They have been with you for years because you know their situation well. The system should prioritize outreach to these clients, not just your biggest accounts.

**Google Business Profile optimization.** Your Google listing is where prospects find you. A good system not only generates reviews but also keeps your profile updated with posts, photos, and accurate service information.

## Your Questions Answered

**Do I need to worry about state board rules when soliciting reviews?**
Automated review requests are standard practice and do not create compliance issues as long as the messaging is professional and you are not offering incentives for specific ratings. We design requests that reflect the professionalism your clients expect from a CPA.

**What if I only have a few reviews right now?**
That is actually a good starting point. Research shows that firms see meaningful search visibility improvements once they reach 10 to 15 quality reviews. An automated system can help you reach that threshold within a few months, then keep building from there.

**Should I focus on Google reviews or other platforms too?**
Google is the priority because it directly impacts local search rankings and is where the vast majority of prospects find and evaluate CPAs. We build systems focused on Google Business Profile reviews. That is where the ROI is highest and where consistent effort compounds over time.

**Will review requests go out during busy season?**
Yes, and that is actually ideal. Busy season is when you have the most client interactions and the highest volume of completed returns. The system can be configured to send requests right after returns are accepted, which is when client satisfaction is at its peak.
