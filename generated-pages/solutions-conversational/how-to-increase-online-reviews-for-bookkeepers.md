Learning how to increase online reviews as a bookkeeper starts with understanding what makes your situation different. You have a smaller, closer client base than most accounting professionals. ## The Bookkeeper's Review Challenge Is Different

Tax preparers can ask for a review after filing a return. That is a clear milestone. But when does a bookkeeper ask? After reconciling March? After the third month of service? After catching an error that saved the client money?

The ongoing nature of bookkeeping means there is no single obvious moment. But there are natural windows:

**After a strong month-end close.** When the books are clean and the client sees organized financials, that is a moment of appreciation.

**After catching a costly mistake.** If you find a billing error, a missed deduction, or a cash flow problem before it becomes a crisis, the client feels the value of what you do. That gratitude is a review opportunity.

**At the quarterly or annual check-in.** If you have a regular meeting cadence, the end of a productive check-in is a natural time for a review request.

**After onboarding.** A new client who just experienced your setup process and felt well taken care of is a strong candidate for an early review.

The key is building these triggers into a system rather than trying to remember them yourself.

## Why Pacing Matters More for Bookkeepers

Here is something that affects bookkeepers specifically: if you have 20 to 30 clients and you ask all of them for reviews in the same week, Google may flag that as suspicious activity. A sudden spike of reviews in a short period can result in reviews being filtered or delayed.

With a smaller client base, you need to stagger your requests over time. One or two per week is a sustainable pace that looks organic to Google. An automated system handles this pacing naturally.

Your goal should be reaching 15 quality reviews as a first milestone. That is the threshold where research shows businesses start attracting noticeably more prospects from search. From there, aim for 50 to 60 over time.

## Video Testimonials: A Bookkeeper's Secret Weapon

Because your client relationships are ongoing and personal, you are in a better position than most professionals to get video testimonials. A 30-second video of a small business owner saying "my bookkeeper saved me hours every month" is worth more than ten written reviews.

Video testimonials from real business owners are the most effective form of social proof, and bookkeepers are uniquely positioned to get them because of the trust and familiarity built through regular interactions.

You do not need professional production. A client recording on their phone and emailing it to you is more than enough. The authenticity is what makes it work.

## Your Questions Answered

**I only have 15 to 25 clients. Is it even worth automating?**
Yes, and actually your smaller base is an advantage. You have deep relationships with every client, which means higher response rates per request. The automation is not about volume. It is about consistency and timing. Even getting one review per month adds up to a strong profile within a year.

**Is Google the only platform that matters?**
For search visibility, yes. Google reviews directly impact your local search rankings and are the first thing prospects see when they search for a bookkeeper. That is where we focus our efforts. A strong Google review profile does more for your business than spreading reviews thinly across multiple platforms.

**What if asking for a review feels awkward with ongoing clients?**
This is the most common concern bookkeepers have, and it is valid. The system is designed to feel like a natural extension of your service, not a sales ask. A message like "We just wrapped up a great quarter together. If you have 30 seconds, a quick Google review would mean a lot to us" fits the tone of an ongoing relationship without feeling pushy.

**How do I handle a client who has a complaint?**
If a client has an unresolved issue, the system should not send them a review request. Smart segmentation excludes clients who have open support tickets or recent negative interactions. If a negative review does come in, responding professionally and quickly shows prospects that you take feedback seriously and work to resolve it.
