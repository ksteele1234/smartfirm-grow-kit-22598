## The Leaky Bucket Problem

Imagine you spend $2,000 a month on marketing. Your website generates 15 inquiries. Of those 15, you respond to 10 quickly, 3 get delayed responses, and 2 get no response at all. Of the 10 you respond to, you follow up consistently with 4. The others get one conversation and then silence.

At the end of the month, you closed 2 new clients. You look at the numbers and think "marketing is not working." But marketing generated 15 leads. Your lead management process lost 13 of them.

This is the leaky bucket. The money and effort you put into generating leads gets wasted when there is no system to capture, track, and nurture every prospect through to a decision.

---

## Where Leads Get Lost

There are three stages where accounting firms typically lose leads:

**Stage 1: The initial response gap.** Prospect fills out a form or calls. Nobody responds for 24 to 48 hours. By then, they have contacted other firms. Studies show that waiting more than 24 hours to follow up drastically reduces your chance of connecting.

**Stage 2: The "let me think about it" void.** You have a good first conversation. The prospect says they need to think about it. You say "great, let me know." Then nobody follows up. The prospect forgets. Three months later, they hire someone else.

**Stage 3: The lost-in-email abyss.** The prospect is somewhere in your inbox, mixed in with 200 client emails. You meant to follow up but cannot find the thread. There is no system tracking who needs attention and when.

Each of these stages has a specific fix, and all three can be automated.

---

## Spreadsheets and Sticky Notes Are Not a System

If you are tracking prospects in a spreadsheet, a notebook, or your memory, you are losing leads. Period. These approaches fail because they require manual effort to maintain, and manual effort is the first thing that drops when you get busy.

A lead management system does three things your spreadsheet cannot:

**Automatic capture.** Every inquiry from every source (website, phone, email, referral) gets logged automatically. No lead goes unrecorded.

**Stage tracking.** Each prospect has a visible status: new, contacted, in conversation, proposal sent, closed, lost. You can see your entire pipeline at a glance.

**Triggered follow-up.** When a prospect sits in a stage too long without activity, the system sends a follow-up message or alerts a team member. No lead goes stale because someone forgot.

## Your Questions Answered

**Do we need to buy expensive CRM software?**
Not necessarily. The right CRM depends on your firm's size, complexity, and existing tools. Some firms do well with simple, affordable options. Others need more robust platforms. We assess your situation during discovery and recommend a system that fits your needs and budget. The most expensive option is not always the best one for accounting firms.

**We are too small for a formal sales process. Is this overkill?**
If you have prospects contacting you and you are not tracking them systematically, you are losing some of them. That is true whether you get 5 inquiries a month or 50. A simple system with automated capture, basic stage tracking, and follow-up reminders is not overkill. It is minimum viable lead management.

**How is this different from just being more disciplined about follow-up?**
Discipline works until busy season, until a big project hits, until a partner goes on vacation. Systems work all the time. The goal is not to rely on human memory and motivation for something this important to your firm's growth.

**What if our leads are coming from referrals, not marketing?**
Referral leads need tracking and follow-up just as much as marketing leads. In fact, mishandling a referral lead is worse because it damages the relationship with the person who referred them. The system captures and tracks all leads regardless of source.
