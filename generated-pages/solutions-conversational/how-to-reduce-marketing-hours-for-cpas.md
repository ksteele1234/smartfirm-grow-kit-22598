# How to Reduce Marketing Hours for CPAs

The fastest way to reduce marketing hours is not to do less marketing. It is to stop doing marketing activities that produce nothing and automate the ones that actually work. Most CPAs spend time on the wrong things, which makes all marketing feel like a waste.

## The Marketing Time Audit

Before you can reduce your marketing hours, you need to know where they are going. Here is what we typically find when we audit a CPA's marketing time:

**Social media: 3 to 5 hours per week.** Posting on social media, checking engagement, responding to comments. For most CPA firms, the return on this time is close to zero. Unless you have a clear niche audience on a specific platform, social media generates awareness but almost no clients.

**Networking and events: 2 to 4 hours per week.** Attending local business groups, chamber meetings, and professional events. Some of these produce referrals. Most produce business card exchanges that go nowhere.

**Content creation: 1 to 3 hours per week (sporadic).** Writing blog posts, creating newsletters, developing resources. Often started with enthusiasm and abandoned within a few months because the results are not visible yet.

**Administrative marketing tasks: 1 to 2 hours per week.** Updating listings, managing website, coordinating with vendors. Low-value tasks that eat time without producing leads.

**Total: 7 to 14 hours per week on marketing.** Of that, maybe 2 to 3 hours are spent on activities that actually generate clients. The rest is motion without progress.

---

## Cut, Automate, or Delegate

Once you know where the time goes, every activity falls into one of three categories:

**Cut it.** If an activity has not generated a client or meaningful lead in the last 6 months, stop doing it. For most CPAs, this means reducing or eliminating general social media posting and most networking events. This alone can save 4 to 7 hours per week.

**Automate it.** Activities that produce results but require manual effort are automation candidates. Email follow-up sequences. Review requests. Google Business Profile posts. Lead capture and tracking. Appointment scheduling. These produce real value but do not need a human doing them every time.

**Delegate it.** Content creation and SEO strategy produce long-term results but require consistent execution. Done-for-you services handle these without your involvement beyond providing direction and approval.

The goal is to go from 7 to 14 hours per week of scattered effort to a system that runs with minimal input and produces better results.

---

## The Activities Worth Keeping

Not everything should be cut or automated. A few marketing activities benefit from your personal involvement:

**Discovery calls with qualified prospects.** This is where your expertise converts interest into engagement. No system replaces a real conversation with a knowledgeable CPA. But the system should be delivering qualified, warmed-up prospects to these calls instead of you spending hours finding them yourself.

**Strategic relationships.** If you have a specific attorney, financial advisor, or business consultant who sends you quality referrals, investing time in that relationship is worth it. But this is one or two relationships, not five networking events a month.

**Thought leadership (optional).** If you genuinely enjoy writing or speaking about your area of expertise, it can be a powerful differentiator. But it should be optional, not the foundation of your marketing plan. The automated system should generate clients whether or not you write a single article.

## Your Questions Answered

**What if social media is actually working for us?**
If you can point to clients who found you through social media, by all means keep it. Most CPAs cannot. The recommendation to cut social media is based on what we see across the profession, not a universal rule. If your data shows it works, the system can incorporate it.

**Will cutting back on networking hurt our referrals?**
Selective networking is different from eliminating it. If a specific group or relationship generates referrals, keep investing in it. But if you are attending three events a week out of a vague sense of "I should be out there," that time is better spent elsewhere. Quality over quantity applies to networking as much as anything else.

**How quickly will we see results from this approach?**
Cutting low-value activities frees up time immediately. Automation improvements typically show results within the first month or two. SEO and content are longer-term investments that compound over three to six months. The time savings are instant. The revenue impact builds over time.

**What if we are not sure which activities are producing results?**
That is exactly why we start with an audit. Most CPA firms have no tracking in place, so they cannot tell which activities generate clients and which do not. Setting up basic attribution is one of the first things we do, so future decisions are based on data instead of gut feeling.
