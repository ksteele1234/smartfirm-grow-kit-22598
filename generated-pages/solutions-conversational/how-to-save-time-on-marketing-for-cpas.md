## The Billable Hour Trap

CPAs think in billable hours. That is how you were trained and how your practice generates revenue. Every hour spent on marketing is an hour not billed to clients.

This creates a mental block. When you sit down to write a blog post or update your Google listing, a voice in your head says "I should be doing client work right now." So marketing gets pushed to evenings, weekends, or the mythical "when things slow down" that never arrives.

The math makes the situation worse. If your effective rate is $250 an hour, a 3-hour marketing session "costs" $750 in foregone billable work. That is a real economic cost, and it is why DIY marketing rarely makes sense for CPAs.

The solution is not to find more time. It is to remove yourself from the process entirely.

---

## What CPAs Actually Spend Time On (And What Is Wasted)

When most CPAs say they "spend time on marketing," here is what they actually mean:

**Posting on social media (2 to 3 hours/week).** The ROI for accounting firm social media is typically very low. Unless you are creating genuinely educational content for a targeted audience, social media posts generate almost no new clients for CPAs. This is usually the first thing to cut.

**Updating the website (sporadic).** Making occasional tweaks to your website feels productive but rarely moves the needle. What matters is whether your site converts visitors into inquiries, not whether you changed the homepage photo.

**Networking events (3 to 5 hours/week).** Valuable for referrals but wildly inconsistent in results. Some events yield clients. Most yield business cards that sit on your desk.

**Thinking about marketing without doing it (constant).** The mental overhead of knowing you "should be marketing" is itself a time cost. It creates guilt and distraction even during billable work.

A done-for-you system eliminates all four of these. The system handles the actual marketing work. You stop spending time on low-ROI activities. And the mental burden lifts because you know it is being handled.

## The 90/10 Rule of CPA Marketing

Roughly 90% of new client acquisition for CPAs comes from three activities:

**Google search visibility.** When someone searches for a CPA in your area or for a service you offer, you need to appear. This requires ongoing SEO work, but it does not require your time specifically.

**Google reviews.** A strong review profile builds immediate trust with prospects. Review generation can be fully automated.

**Lead follow-up.** Most CPAs lose more leads to poor follow-up than to poor marketing. Automated nurture sequences convert prospects you are already generating.

The other 90% of marketing activities that CPAs try (social media, print ads, sponsorships, networking) produce roughly 10% of the results. Cutting those and focusing on the three that matter is how you save time and get better results simultaneously.

Keep doing them. The goal is not to eliminate everything you enjoy. It is to eliminate the time you spend on low-return activities so that your optional marketing efforts (like networking events you value) are a bonus, not the entire strategy.
